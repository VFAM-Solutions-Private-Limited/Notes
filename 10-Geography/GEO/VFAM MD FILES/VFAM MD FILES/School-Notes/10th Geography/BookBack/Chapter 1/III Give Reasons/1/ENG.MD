### 1. The Himalayas are called **Young Fold Mountains**
 
- The Himalayas were formed only a **few million years ago** — so they are very young compared to other mountains.
- They were formed when the **Eurasian land mass** (in the north) and **Gondwana land mass** (in the south) moved towards each other.
- The **Tethys Sea** between them got squeezed and **folded upward** to form the Himalayas.
- Because they were formed by **folding of Earth's crust**, they are called **fold mountains**.
- Since they are geologically young and still rising, they are called **young fold mountains**.
