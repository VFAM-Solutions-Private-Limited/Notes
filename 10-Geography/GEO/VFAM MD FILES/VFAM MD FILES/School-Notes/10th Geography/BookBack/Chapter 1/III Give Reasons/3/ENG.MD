### 3. South Indian Rivers are **East Flowing**
 
- Most South Indian (Peninsular) rivers originate in the **Western Ghats**.
- The **Western Ghats** are close to the west coast, so the land **slopes from west to east**.
- Rivers always flow from **higher areas to lower areas**.
- Since the **eastern side is lower**, all major peninsular rivers like **Godavari, Krishna, Kaveri, Mahanadi** flow eastward.
- They finally **empty into the Bay of Bengal** (which is in the east).
- *(Exception: Narmada and Tapti flow westward through rift valleys)*
