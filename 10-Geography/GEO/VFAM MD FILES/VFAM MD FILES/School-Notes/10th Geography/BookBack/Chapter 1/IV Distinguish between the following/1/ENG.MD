### 1. Himalayan Rivers vs Peninsular Rivers
 
| Feature | **Himalayan Rivers** | **Peninsular Rivers** |
|---|---|---|
| **Origin** | From Himalayan glaciers | From Western Ghats or plateau |
| **Nature** | Perennial (flow all year) | Seasonal (dry in summer) |
| **Length** | Long and wide | Short and narrow |
| **Water source** | Glaciers + Rainfall | Only Rainfall |
| **Valley** | Deep gorges in upper course | Broad and shallow valleys |
| **Delta** | Form large deltas (Sundarbans) | Also form deltas |
| **Examples** | Indus, Ganga, Brahmaputra | Godavari, Krishna, Kaveri |
| **Direction** | Generally flow east/south | Mostly east; some west |
| **Hydro power** | Less suitable | More suitable |
| **Navigation** | Middle and lower courses navigable | Not useful for navigation |
