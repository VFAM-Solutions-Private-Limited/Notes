### 1. Divisions of Northern Mountains and Importance to India
 
The **Northern Mountains** stretch about **2500 km** from the Indus Gorge (west) to the Brahmaputra Gorge (east). They are divided into **three main parts**:
 
#### (A) Trans-Himalayas (Western Himalayas)
- Located **north of the Himalayas**, in Jammu & Kashmir and Tibet.
- Also called **Tibetan Himalayas** as most of the area is in Tibet.
- Important ranges: **Zaskar, Ladakh, Kailash, Karakoram**.
- Width: 40 km at edges, 225 km in the centre.
#### (B) The Himalayas (Central Himalayas)
These are further divided into **three sub-ranges**:
 
**i) Greater Himalayas (Himadri)**
- Highest range with average height of **6000 m**.
- Contains highest peaks: **Mt. Everest (8848 m)** and **Kanchenjunga (8586 m)**.
- Always covered with **permanent snow** — source of glaciers like Gangotri and Yamunotri.
**ii) Lesser Himalayas (Himachal)**
- Middle range with height **3700–4500 m**.
- Famous **hill stations**: Shimla, Mussoorie, Nainital, Darjeeling.
- Ranges: Pir Panjal, Dhauladhar, Mahabharat.
**iii) Outer Himalayas (Siwaliks)**
- Lowest range, height **900–1100 m**.
- Valleys between Siwaliks and Lesser Himalayas are called **Duns** (west) and **Duars** (east).
#### (C) Purvanchal Hills (Eastern Himalayas)
- Eastern extension of Himalayas through **North-East India**.
- Includes: Naga Hills, Khasi Hills, Garo Hills, Manipur Hills, etc.
#### **Importance of Himalayas:**
- **Blocks cold winds** from Central Asia — protects India from severe cold.
- **Forces monsoon winds** to rise — causes heavy rainfall in North India.
- **Natural barrier** — protects India from invasions.
- **Source of perennial rivers** — Indus, Ganga, Brahmaputra.
- **Rich biodiversity** — home to rare plants and animals.
- **Hill stations and pilgrimage centres** — Amarnath, Kedarnath, Badrinath, Vaishno Devi.
- Provides **raw materials** for forest-based industries.
- Famous **tourist paradise**.
