### 2. Major Peninsular Rivers of India
 
The Peninsular rivers originate mostly from the **Western Ghats** and are **seasonal (non-perennial)** rivers. They are divided into **east-flowing** and **west-flowing** rivers.
 
#### **East Flowing Rivers:**
 
**i) Mahanadi**
- Originates near **Sihawa, Raipur district, Chhattisgarh**.
- Length: **851 km** — flows through **Odisha**.
- Empties into **Bay of Bengal**.
- Forms one of the **largest deltas** in India (Sundarban delta area).

**ii) Godavari**
- Longest peninsular river — **1465 km**.
- Also called **"Vridha Ganga"** (Old Ganga).
- Originates in **Nasik district, Maharashtra** (Western Ghats).
- Flows through **Telangana and Andhra Pradesh** → Bay of Bengal.
- Splits into **Vasishta and Gautami** near Rajahmundry.

**iii) Krishna**
- Second longest peninsular river — **1400 km**.
- Originates at **Mahabaleshwar, Maharashtra** (Western Ghats).
- Flows through **Andhra Pradesh** → Bay of Bengal at **Hamasaladeevi**.
- Major tributaries: Bhima, Tungabhadra, Musi, Koyna.

**iv) Kaveri (Cauvery)**
- Originates at **Talakaveri, Kodagu Hills, Karnataka**.
- Length: **800 km** — called **"Dakshina Ganga"** (Ganga of South).
- Forms **Sivasamudram** island in Karnataka, **Hogenakkal Falls** in Tamil Nadu.
- Finally empties into Bay of Bengal at **Poompuhar**.
#### **West Flowing Rivers:**
 
**v) Narmada**
- Originates at **Amarkantak Plateau, MP** (1057 m height).
- Length: **1312 km** — largest west-flowing river in Peninsular India.
- Empties into Arabian Sea through **Gulf of Cambay**.
**vi) Tapti (Tapi)**
- Originates in **Betul district, MP** (752 m height).
- Length: **724 km**.
- Empties into Arabian Sea through **Gulf of Cambay**.
