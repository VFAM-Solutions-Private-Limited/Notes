### 3. Detailed Account on the Basin of the Ganga
 
The **Ganga river system** is the **largest drainage system in India**, covering an area of **8,61,404 sq.km**.
 
#### **Origin:**
- The Ganga originates as **Bhagirathi** from the **Gangotri Glacier** in Uttarkhand (at **7010 m** height).
- It starts in **Uttar Khasi District of Uttarkhand**.
#### **Course and Length:**
- Total length: **2525 km**.
- It flows through **Uttarkhand, Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, West Bengal**, then enters **Bangladesh**.
- In Bangladesh, it is called **Padma**.
#### **Major Tributaries:**
 
| From North (left bank) | From South (right bank) |
|---|---|
| Gomti | Yamuna (**largest tributary**) |
| Ghaghra | Son |
| Gandak | Chambal |
| Kosi | Betwa |
 
#### **The Great Ganga Plain:**
- The Ganga plain is the **most densely populated** region in India.
- Many important cities are on the banks: **Haridwar, Allahabad (Prayagraj), Varanasi, Patna, Kolkata**.
#### **Delta:**
- The Ganga and Brahmaputra together form the **Sundarbans Delta** — the **world's largest delta** — in Bangladesh.
- It then empties into the **Bay of Bengal**.
#### **Importance:**
- Supports **millions of farmers** with fertile alluvial soil.
- Used for **drinking water** by hundreds of millions.
- Important for **navigation** in its middle and lower course.
- Has deep **religious and cultural significance** for Indians.
- The plain is the **heart of Indian civilization**.
