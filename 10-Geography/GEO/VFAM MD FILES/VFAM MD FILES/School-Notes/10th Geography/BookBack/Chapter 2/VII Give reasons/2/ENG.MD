### 2. **Mountains are cooler than plains** — Why?
 
- As we go **higher in altitude**, the atmosphere becomes **thinner and less dense**.
- Thinner air cannot hold heat well, so temperatures **drop as we go up**.
- The temperature decreases at the rate of **6.5°C for every 1000 metres** of height — this is the **Normal Lapse Rate**.
- So mountain areas like **Ooty, Shimla, Mussoorie** are much cooler than nearby plains like **Chennai, Delhi, Lucknow**.
- Also, higher areas receive **less direct solar radiation** as the sun's rays are more slanted.
- That is why **mountains are always cooler than the plains** at the same latitude.
