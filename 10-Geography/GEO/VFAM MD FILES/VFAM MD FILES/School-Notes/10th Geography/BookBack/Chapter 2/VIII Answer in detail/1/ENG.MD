### 1. Write about the **Southwest Monsoon**
 
The **Southwest Monsoon** is the most significant feature of the **Indian climate**. It brings about **75% of India's annual rainfall**.
 
#### **Onset:**
- The Southwest Monsoon normally begins at the **southern tip of India** (Kerala) in the **first week of June**.
- It advances along the **Konkan coast** in early June.
- It covers the **entire country by 15th July**.
#### **Burst of Monsoon:**
- Before the monsoon arrives, temperature in North India reaches **46°C**.
- The sudden arrival of monsoon with **thunder and lightning** is called **"Burst of Monsoon"**.
#### **Two Branches:**
When the monsoon strikes the southern tip of India, it divides into **two branches**:
 
**i) Arabian Sea Branch:**
- Gives **heavy rainfall** to the **west coast** (windward side of Western Ghats).
- Advances northward and is blocked by the **Himalayas** → causes heavy rain in North India.
- **Aravalli Mountains** are parallel to wind direction → so **Rajasthan gets less rain**.
**ii) Bay of Bengal Branch:**
- Moves towards **Northeast India and Myanmar**.
- Trapped by **Garo, Khasi and Jaintia hills** → causes heaviest rainfall at **Mawsynram** (Meghalaya) — **1141 cm/year** — highest in the world.
- As this wind moves westward, rainfall **decreases from east to west**.
#### **Influence of El Niño:**
- The Southwest Monsoon is also influenced by the global phenomenon called **El Niño**, which can weaken the monsoon and cause drought conditions.
#### **Importance:**
- Brings rain to **most of India**.
- Supports **agriculture** for millions of farmers.
- Fills **rivers, lakes and reservoirs**.
- Controls **India's weather pattern** for most of the year.
