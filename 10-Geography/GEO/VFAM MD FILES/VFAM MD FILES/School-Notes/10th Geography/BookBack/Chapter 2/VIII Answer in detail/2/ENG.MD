### 2. Describe the **Forests of India**
 
India has several types of natural forests based on rainfall, temperature and altitude:
 
#### **1. Tropical Evergreen Forests**
- **Rainfall**: More than **200 cm**
- **Temperature**: Above 22°C, **Humidity**: Above 70%
- **Location**: Western Ghats (Maharashtra, Karnataka, Kerala), Andaman & Nicobar, Assam, Meghalaya
- **Key trees**: Rubber, Rosewood, Bamboo, Cinchona, Cedar
- These forests stay **green throughout the year** (do not shed leaves).
#### **2. Tropical Deciduous Forests (Monsoon Forests)**
- **Rainfall**: **100–200 cm**
- **Temperature**: About 27°C
- **Location**: Sub-Himalayan region, Punjab to Assam, Great Plains, Central India, South India
- **Key trees**: **Teak and Sal** (most important), Sandalwood, Bamboo, Rosewood
- Trees **shed leaves** during spring and early summer.
- Provide fragrant oils, varnish, sandalwood oil.
#### **3. Tropical Dry Forests**
- **Rainfall**: **50–100 cm**
- **Location**: East Rajasthan, Haryana, Punjab, Western UP, Madhya Pradesh, East Maharashtra.
- **Key trees**: Banyan, Bamboo, Babool, Kikar
#### **4. Desert and Semi-desert Vegetation (Tropical Thorn Forests)**
- **Rainfall**: Less than **50 cm**
- **Location**: Northwest India — West Rajasthan, SW Haryana, North Gujarat, SW Punjab; also dry parts of Deccan Plateau
- **Key trees**: Babul, Kikar, Wild Palms
- Low humidity, high temperature.
#### **5. Alpine / Himalayan Forests**
- Found at **different altitudes** in the Himalayas:
  - **900–1800 m**: Chir pine trees
  - **1200–2400 m** (NE states): Sal, Oak, Chestnut, Cinnamon
  - **2400–3600 m**: Oak, Birch, Silver Fir, Spruce, Juniper
  - **Above 2400 m (Alpine)**: Only coniferous trees — Oak, Silver Fir, Pine, Juniper
- Found in: Jammu & Kashmir, Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand, NE states
#### **6. Mangrove Forests (Delta/Swamp Forests)**
- Found in **deltas, estuaries and creeks** affected by tides.
- **Location**: Sundarbans (Ganga-Brahmaputra Delta) — **world's largest tidal forest**; also Mahanadi, Godavari, Krishna deltas.
- These forests are also called **Sundarbans forest**.
