### 2. Solutions for Water Disputes in South India
 
Water disputes in South India (mainly the Cauvery river dispute between Tamil Nadu and Karnataka) are a very serious problem. Here are some possible solutions:
 
- **Rainwater Harvesting:** Every district and village should build proper rainwater collection systems (check dams, tanks, rooftop harvesting). This reduces dependence on river water.
- **Drip Irrigation:** Farmers should be encouraged to use drip irrigation and sprinkler methods which save up to 70% of water. Less water wasted means less dispute.
- **Interlinking of Rivers:** Linking rivers that have surplus water with rivers that have less water can distribute water more evenly across states.
- **Permanent Tribunal:** A permanent and powerful water disputes tribunal should be set up by the Central Government with the authority to enforce its decisions quickly.
- **Wastewater Recycling:** Cities should treat and reuse wastewater for industrial and agricultural purposes so that fresh river water is saved.
- **Cropping Pattern Change:** Farmers in water-scarce areas should shift from water-intensive crops like paddy and sugarcane to less water-intensive crops like millets, pulses, and vegetables.
- **Cooperative Management:** All states sharing a river should jointly manage it through a permanent inter-state water authority with representation from all states.
- **Public Awareness:** People should be educated about water conservation from school level so that future generations use water more responsibly.
Water is a shared resource. States must learn to cooperate and share it fairly rather than fight over it.
