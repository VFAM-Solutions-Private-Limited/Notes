### 5. Types of Agriculture Practices in India
 
There are **6 main types** of farming in India:
 
1. **Subsistence Farming** - Growing crops only for the family's own food
2. **Shifting Agriculture** - Clearing forest land, farming for 2-3 years, then moving to new land (also called Slash and Burn farming)
3. **Intensive Farming** - Using machines, fertilizers, and pesticides to get maximum output from small land
4. **Dry Farming** - Farming in dry areas without irrigation; only drought-resistant crops are grown
5. **Mixed Farming** - Combining crop farming with animal rearing, poultry, fishery, etc.
6. **Terrace Farming** - Cutting hill slopes into steps/terraces to grow crops on hilly land
