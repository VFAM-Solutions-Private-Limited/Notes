### Inundational Canal and Perennial Canal
 
| **Inundational Canal** | **Perennial Canal** |
|---|---|
| In this, water is taken out directly from the rivers without making any kind of barrage or dam. | These are developed from perennial rivers by constructing barrages to regulate the flow of water. |
| These canals are not useful for irrigation. | These canals are useful for irrigation. |
| Punjab has got a large number of inundation canals drawing water from the Sutlej river. | These canals are found in Punjab, Haryana and Uttar Pradesh. |
| Inundation canals are floodwater canals that carry water only during the rainy season when the river floods. | Perennial canals carry water all year round, as they have a weir or dam at the head to regulate the flow of water. |
