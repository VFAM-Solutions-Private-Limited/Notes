### 4. Alluvial Soils and Black Soils
 
| Point | **Alluvial Soil** | **Black Soil** |
|---|---|---|
| **How formed** | Deposited by rivers (sand, silt, clay) | Formed from Deccan basalt rocks |
| **Colour** | Light grey to dark grey | Deep black |
| **Main minerals** | Rich in potash, phosphoric acid, lime | Rich in calcium, magnesium, iron, aluminium |
| **What is lacking** | Poor in nitrogen | Poor in nitrogen, phosphoric acid, humus |
| **Water holding** | Moderate | Very high - holds moisture for long time |
| **Nature when wet** | Sandy and silty | Becomes very sticky |
| **Main crops** | Rice, Wheat, Sugarcane, Oilseeds | Cotton, Millets, Tobacco, Sugarcane |
| **Location** | Ganga and Brahmaputra plains, East coast | Maharashtra, Malwa plateau, Telangana |
| **Other name** | Khadar (new) and Bhangar (old) | Regur soil or Cotton soil |
