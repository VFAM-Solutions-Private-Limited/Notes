### 1. Five Types of Soil in India - Characteristics and Distribution
 
India has 8 types of soils classified by ICAR. Here are 5 important types:
 
**1. Alluvial Soil:**
Alluvial soil is the most widespread and fertile soil in India. It is formed by the deposition of sand, silt, and clay brought by rivers. It is rich in potash, phosphoric acid, and lime but poor in nitrogen. It is found in the Ganga and Brahmaputra river valleys and plains of Uttar Pradesh, Punjab, Haryana, and West Bengal. Major crops grown are rice, wheat, sugarcane, and oilseeds.
 
**2. Black Soil (Regur Soil):**
Black soil is formed from the weathering of Deccan basalt rocks. It is black in colour due to the presence of titanium and iron. Its most important feature is its high ability to hold moisture - it becomes sticky when wet and cracks when dry. It is rich in calcium and magnesium but poor in nitrogen and humus. It is found in Maharashtra, Malwa Plateau, Telangana, and parts of Andhra Pradesh and Karnataka. Cotton grows best in this soil, so it is also called cotton soil.
 
**3. Red Soil:**
Red soil is formed by the decomposition of old crystalline rocks like granite and gneiss. It is red in colour because of the presence of iron. It is poor in nitrogen, humus, and phosphoric acid. It is found in the eastern Deccan Plateau, Tamil Nadu, Kerala, Karnataka, and Jharkhand. Crops like wheat, rice, cotton, and pulses are grown here.
 
**4. Laterite Soil:**
Laterite soil is formed in areas where there is alternating wet and dry climate. It is formed by the process of leaching (where rainwater washes away nutrients). It is mainly composed of iron and aluminium oxides. It is more acidic on higher areas and cannot hold moisture well. It is found in Assam hills, Kerala, Karnataka, Eastern Ghats, and Odisha. Crops like coffee, rubber, cashew, and tapioca grow here.
 
**5. Forest and Mountain Soil:**
This soil is found in hilly and forested regions. It is formed due to mechanical weathering caused by snow, temperature changes, and rain. It is rich in humus but deficient in potash, phosphorus, and lime. It is acidic in nature due to slow decomposition. It is found in Jammu and Kashmir, Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand, and parts of Western and Eastern Ghats. Tea, coffee, potato, barley, and various spices are grown here.
