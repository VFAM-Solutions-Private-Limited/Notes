### 2. Multipurpose Projects - Meaning and Two Examples
 
**What is a Multipurpose River Valley Project?**
 
A multipurpose river valley project is a **dam built across a river** to serve many purposes at the same time. A single dam provides benefits like:
- **Irrigation** for farms
- **Hydro-electric power** generation
- **Drinking water** supply for cities and villages
- **Flood control** to prevent flooding of villages
- **Fishery development**
- **Navigation** (water transport)
These projects are called multipurpose because they solve many problems with one project.
 
**Example 1: Bhakra-Nangal Project**
- Built across the **Sutlej River** in Himachal Pradesh
- It is the **highest gravity dam in the world**
- Benefits the states of **Punjab, Haryana, and Rajasthan**
- Provides massive irrigation water and hydro-electric power
- Transformed Punjab and Haryana into the food bowl of India during the Green Revolution
**Example 2: Hirakud Project**
- Built across the **Mahanadi River** in Odisha
- It is the **longest dam in the world**
- Benefits the state of **Odisha**
- Controls floods in the Mahanadi basin which used to cause great damage every year
- Also provides irrigation water and electricity
