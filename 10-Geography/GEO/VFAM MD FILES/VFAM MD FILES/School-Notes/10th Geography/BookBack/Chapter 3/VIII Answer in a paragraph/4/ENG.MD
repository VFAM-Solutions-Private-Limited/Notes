### 4. Geographical Conditions for Cultivation of Rice and Wheat
 
**Rice:**
 
Rice is a **tropical crop** and needs the following conditions:
 
- **Temperature:** Needs high temperature. Mean temperature of about **24°C** is ideal. It cannot grow in cold regions.
- **Rainfall:** Needs **heavy rainfall of 150 cm or more** per year. Alternatively, it needs irrigation.
- **Soil:** Grows best in **deep, fertile, clayey or loamy soil** that can hold water.
- **Labour:** Rice cultivation, especially transplanting, requires **abundant cheap labour**.
- **Land:** Flat land or terraced hilly land with proper water supply is needed.
- **Leading states:** West Bengal (1st), Uttar Pradesh, Punjab, Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh
**Wheat:**
 
Wheat is a **Rabi (winter) crop** and needs the following conditions:
 
- **Temperature at sowing:** Needs **cool temperature of 10-15°C** when seeds are planted.
- **Temperature at ripening:** Needs **warm temperature of 20-25°C** when the grain is maturing.
- **Rainfall:** Needs **moderate rainfall of 75-100 cm**. Too much rain damages the crop.
- **Soil:** Grows well in **fertile alluvial soil** or well-drained loamy soil. Also grows in black soil areas of Deccan.
- **Topography:** Flat or gently sloping land is ideal.
- **Leading states:** Uttar Pradesh, Punjab, Haryana, Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh (these 5 states produce 85% of India's wheat)
**Key Difference:** Rice needs **hot and wet** conditions while Wheat needs **cool and moderate** conditions.
