### 2. Metallic Minerals and Non-metallic Minerals

| **Metallic Minerals** | **Non-metallic Minerals** |
|---|---|
| These are minerals from which we get metals. They contain metals such as iron, copper and gold. | These minerals do not contain metals. They can be biological (organic) or inorganic in origin. |
| Classified into ferrous (contain iron, e.g., iron ore) and non-ferrous (do not contain iron, e.g., copper, bauxite). | Organic non-metallic minerals (e.g., coal, petroleum) are formed from buried plant and animal remains. Inorganic ones include mica, limestone and graphite. |
| Good conductors of heat and electricity. | Poor conductors of heat and electricity. |
| **Examples:** Iron ore, copper, gold, silver, lead, aluminium and tin. | **Examples:** Coal, petroleum, limestone and mica. |
