### 3. Roadways and Railways.

| **Roadways** | **Railways** |
|---|---|
| Highly suitable for short-distance services. | Ideal for long-distance services. |
| The most common mode of transport, easy, cheap to construct and maintain. | Construction depends upon climatic and physical factors like terrain. Costly when compared with roadways. |
| Based on the construction and maintenance divided into National Highways, State Highways, District Roads, Village Roads, Border Roads, International Highways and Expressways. | On the basis of the width of the track falls under four types: Broad gauge, Meter gauge, Narrow gauge and Light gauge. |
| Second longest network in the world. | Largest in Asia and second-largest in the world. |
| Door-to-door services are possible. | Cannot provide door-to-door services. |
