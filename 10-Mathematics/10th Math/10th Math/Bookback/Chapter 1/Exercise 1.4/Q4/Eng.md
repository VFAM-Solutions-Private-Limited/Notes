# Exercise 1.4 

## Question 4:

Show that the function

```text
f : ℕ → ℕ defined by

f(x) = 2x - 1
```

is **one-one** but **not onto**.

---

## Procedure to Solve (For Reference):

1. Let the images of two natural numbers **a** and **b** be equal.

```text
f(a) = f(b)
```

2. Substitute the given function

```text
f(x) = 2x - 1
```

into the above equation.

3. Simplify the equation and show that

```text
a = b
```

Hence, conclude that the function is **one-one (injective)**.

4. Find the images of a few natural numbers by substituting values in the function.

5. Observe that the images obtained are only **odd natural numbers**.

6. Since even natural numbers are not the images of any element of the domain, conclude that the function is **not onto (surjective)**.

---


## Solution:

#### Given:

```text
f : ℕ → ℕ

f(x) = 2x - 1
```

---

#### To prove that f is one-one:

Assume

```text
f(x₁) = f(x₂)
```

Then,

```text
2x₁ - 1 = 2x₂ - 1
```

Adding 1 on both sides,

```text
2x₁ = 2x₂
```

Dividing both sides by 2,

```text
x₁ = x₂
```

Hence,

```text
f is one-one.
```

---

#### To prove that f is not onto:

Substitute some natural numbers in the function.

```text
f(1) = 2(1) - 1
     = 1

f(2) = 2(2) - 1
     = 3

f(3) = 2(3) - 1
     = 5

f(4) = 2(4) - 1
     = 7

f(5) = 2(5) - 1
     = 9
```

Thus,

```text
Range = {1, 3, 5, 7, 9, ...}
```

The even natural numbers

```text
2, 4, 6, 8, ...
```


are not images of any element of ℕ.

Therefore,

```text
f is not onto.
```



---

#### Conclusion:

```text
Hence, the function

f : ℕ → ℕ

defined by

f(x) = 2x - 1

is one-one but not onto.
```
