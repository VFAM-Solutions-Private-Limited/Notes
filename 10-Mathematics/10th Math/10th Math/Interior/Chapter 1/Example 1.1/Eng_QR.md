# Example 1.1

## Question:

If A = {1,3,5} and B = {2,3}


then

```text
(i) Find A × B and B × A.

(ii) Is A × B = B × A? If not, why?

(iii) Show that n(A × B) = n(B × A) = n(A) × n(B).
```

---


## Solution:

To find: **A × B**, **B × A** and verify **n(A × B) = n(B × A) = n(A) × n(B)**

---

#### (i) Given : A = {1,3,5} , B = {2,3}

```text
A × B = {1,3,5} × {2,3}

      = {(1,2), (1,3), (3,2), (3,3), (5,2), (5,3)}

B × A = {2,3} × {1,3,5}

      = {(2,1), (2,3), (2,5), (3,1), (3,3), (3,5)}
```

---

#### (ii) Is A × B = B × A?

```text
A × B ≠ B × A
```

Because the order of the elements in an ordered pair is important.

For example,

```text
(1,2) ∈ A × B

But

(1,2) ∉ B × A

Similarly,

(2,1) ∈ B × A

But

(2,1) ∉ A × B
```

Therefore,

```text
A × B ≠ B × A
```

---

#### (iii) Show that

```text
n(A × B) = n(B × A) = n(A) × n(B)
```

```text
n(A) = 3

n(B) = 2
```

```text
n(A × B) = 6

n(B × A) = 6
```

```text
n(A) × n(B)

= 3 × 2

= 6
```

Hence,

```text
n(A × B) = n(B × A) = n(A) × n(B)

            = 6
```
