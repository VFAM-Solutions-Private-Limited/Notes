# Exercise 6.1

## Question 1:

```text
Prove the following identities.

(i)

cotθ + tanθ = secθ cosecθ

(ii)

tan⁴θ + tan²θ = sec⁴θ − sec²θ
```

---

## Procedure:

1. Take the LHS of the identity.
2. Simplify the expression step by step.
3. Apply trigonometric identities wherever needed.
4. Obtain the RHS and prove the identity.

---

## Solution:

#### (i)   cotθ + tanθ = secθ cosecθ

To prove:



```text
LHS = cotθ + tanθ

      cosθ     sinθ
    = ───── + ─────
      sinθ     cosθ

      cos²θ + sin²θ
    = ──────────────
         sinθcosθ

           1
    = ─────────
      sinθcosθ

        1       1
    = ───── × ─────
      sinθ    cosθ

    = cosecθ × secθ

    = secθ cosecθ

    = RHS

Hence proved.
```
---
#### (ii)   tan⁴θ + tan²θ = sec⁴θ − sec²θ

To prove:


```text
LHS = tan⁴θ + tan²θ

    = tan²θ(tan²θ + 1)

    = tan²θ sec²θ

    = (sec²θ − 1)sec²θ

    = sec⁴θ − sec²θ

    = RHS

Hence proved.
```
