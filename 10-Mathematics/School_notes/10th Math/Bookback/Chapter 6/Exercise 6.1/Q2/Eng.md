# Exercise 6.1

## Question 2:

```text
Prove the following identities.



          1 - tan²θ
(i)      ─────────────
          cot²θ - 1     = tan²θ



            cosθ
(ii)    ───────────
          1 + sinθ      = secθ - tanθ
```

---

## Procedure:

1. Take the LHS of the identity.
2. Simplify the expression step by step.
3. Apply trigonometric identities wherever needed.
4. Obtain the RHS and prove the identity.

---

## Solution:

#### (i)   

```text
          1 - tan²θ
        ─────────────
          cot²θ - 1     = tan²θ
```

To prove:

```text
          1 - tan²θ
LHS =  ─────────────
          cot²θ - 1

        1 - tan²θ
    =  ─────────────
            1
        ────────────
         tan²θ - 1

        1 - tan²θ
    =  ─────────────
        1 - tan²θ
        ────────────
            tan²θ

                    tan²θ
    = (1 - tan²θ) × ──────────
                    1 - tan²θ

    = tan²θ

    = RHS

Hence proved.
```
---
#### (ii)       
         cosθ
        ──────────  = secθ - tanθ
        1 + sinθ      
To prove:
```text
            cosθ
LHS =  ─────────────
          1 + sinθ

        cosθ(1 - sinθ)
    =  ──────────────────
         1 - sin²θ

        cosθ(1 - sinθ)
    =  ──────────────────
            cos²θ

        1 - sinθ
    =  ───────────
         cosθ

         1       sinθ
    =  ──── - ───────
        cosθ    cosθ

    = secθ - tanθ

    = RHS

Hence proved.
```
