# Exercise 6.1

## Question 3:

Prove the following identities.

### (i)

$$
\sqrt{\frac{1+\sin\theta}{1-\sin\theta}}
=
\sec\theta+\tan\theta
$$

### (ii)

$$
\sqrt{\frac{1+\sin\theta}{1-\sin\theta}}
+
\sqrt{\frac{1-\sin\theta}{1+\sin\theta}}
=
2\sec\theta
$$

---

## Procedure to solve (For reference):

1. Take the LHS of the identity.
2. Simplify the expression step by step.
3. Apply trigonometric identities wherever needed.
4. Obtain the RHS and prove the identity.

---

## Solution:



#### (i)

$$
\sqrt{\frac{1+\sin\theta}{1-\sin\theta}}
=
\sec\theta+\tan\theta
$$

LHS =

$$
\sqrt{\frac{1+\sin\theta}{1-\sin\theta}}
$$

$$
=
\sqrt{
\frac{(1+\sin\theta)(1+\sin\theta)}
{(1-\sin\theta)(1+\sin\theta)}
}
$$

$$
=
\sqrt{
\frac{(1+\sin\theta)^2}
{\cos^2\theta}
}
\qquad
(\because (a+b)(a-b)=a^2-b^2,\;1-\sin^2\theta=\cos^2\theta)
$$

$$
=
\frac{1+\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}
$$

$$
=
\frac{1}{\cos\theta}
+
\frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}
$$

$$
=
\sec\theta+\tan\theta
\qquad
\left(\because \frac{1}{\cos\theta}=\sec\theta,\;
\frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}=\tan\theta\right)
$$

$$
=
\mathrm{RHS}
$$

LHS = RHS

$$
\sqrt{\frac{1+\sin\theta}{1-\sin\theta}}
=
\sec\theta+\tan\theta
$$

Hence Proved.

---

#### (ii)

$$
\sqrt{\frac{1+\sin\theta}{1-\sin\theta}}
+
\sqrt{\frac{1-\sin\theta}{1+\sin\theta}}
=
2\sec\theta
$$

LHS =

$$
\sqrt{\frac{1+\sin\theta}{1-\sin\theta}}
+
\sqrt{\frac{1-\sin\theta}{1+\sin\theta}}
$$

$$
=
(\sec\theta+\tan\theta)
+
(\sec\theta-\tan\theta)
\qquad
(\because \text{From (i)})
$$

$$
=
\sec\theta+\tan\theta+\sec\theta-\tan\theta
$$

$$
=
2\sec\theta
$$

$$
=
\mathrm{RHS}
$$

LHS = RHS

$$
\sqrt{\frac{1+\sin\theta}{1-\sin\theta}}
+
\sqrt{\frac{1-\sin\theta}{1+\sin\theta}}
=
2\sec\theta
$$


Hence Proved.
