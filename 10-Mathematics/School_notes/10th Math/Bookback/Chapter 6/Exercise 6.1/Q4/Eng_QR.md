

# Exercise 6.1

## Question 4:

Prove the following identities.

### (i)

$$
\sec^6\theta
=
\tan^6\theta
+
3\tan^2\theta\sec^2\theta
+
1
$$

### (ii)

$$
(\sin\theta+\sec\theta)^2
+
(\cos\theta+\cosec\theta)^2
=
1+(\sec\theta+\cosec\theta)^2
$$

---

## Solution:

#### (i)

$$
\sec^6\theta
=
\tan^6\theta
+
3\tan^2\theta\sec^2\theta
+
1
$$

LHS =

$$
\sec^6\theta
$$

$$
=
(\sec^2\theta)^3
$$

$$
=
(\tan^2\theta+1)^3
\qquad
\left(\because \sec^2\theta=\tan^2\theta+1\right)
$$

$$
=
\tan^6\theta
+
3\tan^4\theta
+
3\tan^2\theta
+
1
$$

$$
=
\tan^6\theta
+
3\tan^2\theta(\tan^2\theta+1)
+
1
$$

$$
=
\tan^6\theta
+
3\tan^2\theta\sec^2\theta
+
1
\qquad
\left(\because \tan^2\theta+1=\sec^2\theta\right)
$$

$$
=
\mathrm{RHS}
$$

LHS = RHS

$$
\sec^6\theta
=
\tan^6\theta
+
3\tan^2\theta\sec^2\theta
+
1
$$

Hence Proved.

---

#### (ii)

$$
(\sin\theta+\sec\theta)^2
+
(\cos\theta+\cosec\theta)^2
=
1+(\sec\theta+\cosec\theta)^2
$$

LHS =

$$
(\sin\theta+\sec\theta)^2
+
(\cos\theta+\cosec\theta)^2
$$

$$
=
\sin^2\theta
+
\sec^2\theta
+
2\sin\theta\sec\theta
+
\cos^2\theta
+
\cosec^2\theta
+
2\cos\theta\cosec\theta
$$

$$
=
(\sin^2\theta+\cos^2\theta)
+
(\sec^2\theta+\cosec^2\theta)
+
2\left(\frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}\right)
+
2\left(\frac{\cos\theta}{\sin\theta}\right)
$$

$$
=
1
+
\sec^2\theta
+
\cosec^2\theta
+
2\tan\theta
+
2\cot\theta
\qquad
\left(\because \sin^2\theta+\cos^2\theta=1\right)
$$

$$
=
1
+
\sec^2\theta
+
\cosec^2\theta
+
2\sec\theta\cosec\theta
\qquad
\left(\because \tan\theta+\cot\theta=\sec\theta\cosec\theta\right)
$$

$$
=
1
+
(\sec\theta+\cosec\theta)^2
$$

$$
=
\mathrm{RHS}
$$

LHS = RHS

$$
(\sin\theta+\sec\theta)^2
+
(\cos\theta+\cosec\theta)^2
=
1+(\sec\theta+\cosec\theta)^2
$$

Hence Proved.
