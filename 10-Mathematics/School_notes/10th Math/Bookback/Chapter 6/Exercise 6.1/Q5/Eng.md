# Exercise 6.1

## Question 5:

Prove the following identities.

 (i)   sec⁴θ(1 − sin⁴θ) − 2tan²θ = 1

(ii)

$$
\frac{\cot\theta-\cos\theta}{\cot\theta+\cos\theta}
=
\frac{\cosec\theta-1}{\cosec\theta+1}
$$

---

## Procedure:

1. Take the LHS of the identity.
2. Simplify the expression step by step.
3. Apply standard trigonometric identities wherever needed.
4. Obtain the RHS and prove the identity.

---

## Solution:

#### (i)   sec⁴θ(1 − sin⁴θ) − 2tan²θ = 1

To prove:

```text
LHS = sec⁴θ(1 − sin⁴θ) − 2tan²θ

    = sec⁴θ(1 − sin²θ)(1 + sin²θ) − 2tan²θ

    = sec⁴θ(cos²θ)(1 + sin²θ) − 2tan²θ

    = sec²θ(1 + sin²θ) − 2tan²θ

    = sec²θ + sec²θsin²θ − 2tan²θ

    = sec²θ + tan²θ − 2tan²θ

    = sec²θ − tan²θ

    = 1

    = RHS

Hence proved.
```
---
#### (ii)

$$
\frac{\cot\theta-\cos\theta}{\cot\theta+\cos\theta}
=
\frac{\cosec\theta-1}{\cosec\theta+1}
$$

To prove:

```text
          cotθ - cosθ
LHS =  ───────────────
          cotθ + cosθ

        cosθ     cosθ
      ─────── - ─────
        sinθ       1
    = ─────────────────
        cosθ     cosθ
      ─────── + ─────
        sinθ       1

        cosθ(1 - sinθ)
    = ─────────────────
        cosθ(1 + sinθ)

        1 - sinθ
    = ───────────
        1 + sinθ

      (1 - sinθ)(1 - sinθ)
    = ─────────────────────
         1 - sin²θ

      (1 - sinθ)²
    = ────────────
          cos²θ

      (1 - sinθ)²
    = ────────────
      (cosecθsinθ)²

        1 - sinθ
    = ───────────
        1 + sinθ

      cosecθ - 1
    = ────────────
      cosecθ + 1

    = RHS

Hence proved.
```
