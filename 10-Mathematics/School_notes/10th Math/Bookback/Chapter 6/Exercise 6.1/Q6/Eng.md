# Exercise 6.1

## Question 6:

Prove the following identities.

### (i)

$$
\frac{\sin A-\sin B}{\cos A+\cos B}
+
\frac{\cos A-\cos B}{\sin A+\sin B}
=
0
$$

### (ii)

$$
\frac{\sin^3A+\cos^3A}{\sin A+\cos A}
+
\frac{\sin^3A-\cos^3A}{\sin A-\cos A}
=
2
$$

---

## Procedure:

1. Take the LHS of the identity.
2. Apply algebraic and trigonometric identities.
3. Simplify the expression step by step.
4. Obtain the RHS and prove the identity.

---

## Solution:

#### (i)

$$
\frac{\sin A-\sin B}{\cos A+\cos B}
+
\frac{\cos A-\cos B}{\sin A+\sin B}
=
0
$$

To prove:

$$
\begin{aligned}
LHS &=
\frac{\sin A-\sin B}{\cos A+\cos B}
+
\frac{\cos A-\cos B}{\sin A+\sin B}
\\[6pt]
&=
\frac{2\cos\frac{A+B}{2}\sin\frac{A-B}{2}}
     {2\cos\frac{A+B}{2}\cos\frac{A-B}{2}}
+
\frac{-2\sin\frac{A+B}{2}\sin\frac{A-B}{2}}
     {2\sin\frac{A+B}{2}\cos\frac{A-B}{2}}
\\[6pt]
&=
\frac{\sin\frac{A-B}{2}}
     {\cos\frac{A-B}{2}}
-
\frac{\sin\frac{A-B}{2}}
     {\cos\frac{A-B}{2}}
\\[6pt]
&=
\tan\frac{A-B}{2}
-
\tan\frac{A-B}{2}
\\[6pt]
&=
0
\\[6pt]
&=
\text{RHS}
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence proved.

---
#### (ii)

$$
\frac{\sin^3A+\cos^3A}{\sin A+\cos A}
+
\frac{\sin^3A-\cos^3A}{\sin A-\cos A}
=
2
$$

To prove:

$$
\begin{aligned}
LHS
&=
\frac{\sin^3A+\cos^3A}
     {\sin A+\cos A}
+
\frac{\sin^3A-\cos^3A}
     {\sin A-\cos A}
\\[6pt]
&=
(\sin^2A-\sin A\cos A+\cos^2A)
\\
&\quad+
(\sin^2A+\sin A\cos A+\cos^2A)
\\[6pt]
&=
2\sin^2A+2\cos^2A
\\[6pt]
&=
2(\sin^2A+\cos^2A)
\\[6pt]
&=
2(1)
\\[6pt]
&=
2
\\[6pt]
&=
\text{RHS}
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence proved.
