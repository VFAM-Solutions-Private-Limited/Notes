# Exercise 6.1

## Question 7:

Prove the following.

$$
\text{(i) If } \sin\theta+\cos\theta=\sqrt{3},
\text{ then prove that }
\tan\theta+\cot\theta=1.
$$

$$
\text{(ii) If } \sqrt{3}\sin\theta-\cos\theta=0,
\text{ then show that }
\tan3\theta=
\frac{3\tan\theta-\tan^3\theta}
     {1-3\tan^2\theta}.
$$

---

## Procedure:

1. Write the given condition.
2. Simplify the given expression.
3. Apply the required trigonometric identities.
4. Obtain the required result.

---

## Solution:

#### (i)

Given:

$$
\sin\theta+\cos\theta=\sqrt{3}
$$

To prove:

$$
\tan\theta+\cot\theta=1
$$

$$
(\sin\theta+\cos\theta)^2=3
$$

$$
\sin^2\theta+\cos^2\theta+2\sin\theta\cos\theta=3
$$

$$
1+2\sin\theta\cos\theta=3
$$

$$
2\sin\theta\cos\theta=2
$$

$$
\sin\theta\cos\theta=1
$$

Now,

$$
\tan\theta+\cot\theta
=
\frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}
+
\frac{\cos\theta}{\sin\theta}
$$

$$
=
\frac{\sin^2\theta+\cos^2\theta}
     {\sin\theta\cos\theta}
$$

$$
=
\frac{1}
     {\sin\theta\cos\theta}
$$

$$
=
1
$$

$$
\therefore \tan\theta+\cot\theta=1
$$

Hence proved.

---
#### (ii)

Given:

$$
\sqrt{3}\sin\theta-\cos\theta=0
$$

To prove:

$$
\tan3\theta=
\frac{3\tan\theta-\tan^3\theta}
     {1-3\tan^2\theta}
$$

$$
\sqrt{3}\sin\theta=\cos\theta
$$

$$
\tan\theta=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}
$$

Using the triple angle identity,

$$
\tan3\theta=
\frac{3\tan\theta-\tan^3\theta}
     {1-3\tan^2\theta}
$$

Substituting

$$
\tan\theta=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}
$$

$$
\tan3\theta=
\frac{3\left(\frac1{\sqrt3}\right)-\left(\frac1{\sqrt3}\right)^3}
     {1-3\left(\frac1{\sqrt3}\right)^2}
$$

Hence,

$$
\tan3\theta=
\frac{3\tan\theta-\tan^3\theta}
     {1-3\tan^2\theta}
$$

Hence proved.
