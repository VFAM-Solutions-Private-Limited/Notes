# Exercise 6.2

## Question 1:

Find the angle of elevation of the top of a tower from a point on the ground, which is 30 m away from the foot of a tower of height $10\sqrt{3}$ m.

---

## Procedure:

1. Draw a right-angled triangle representing the tower and the ground.
2. Identify the opposite side and adjacent side.
3. Apply the tangent ratio:

$$
\tan\theta=
\frac{\text{Opposite side}}
{\text{Adjacent side}}
$$

4. Find $\theta$.

## Solution:

Let

$AB = 10\sqrt{3}$ m be the height of the tower.

$BC = 30$ m be the distance from the point to the foot of the tower.

$\angle C = \theta$ be the angle of elevation.

In right $\triangle ABC$,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\tan\theta
&= \frac{AB}{BC}
\\[4pt]
&= \frac{10\sqrt{3}}{30}
\\[4pt]
&= \frac{\sqrt{3}}{3}
\\[4pt]
&= \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}
\end{aligned}
$$

Since

$$
\tan 30^\circ=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}},
$$

therefore,

$$
\theta = 30^\circ
$$

Hence,

$$
\boxed{\text{Angle of elevation }=30^\circ}
$$

Hence proved.
