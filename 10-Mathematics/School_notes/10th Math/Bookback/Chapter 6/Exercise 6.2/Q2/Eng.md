# Exercise 6.2

## Question 2:

A road is flanked on either side by continuous rows of houses of height $4\sqrt{3}$ m with no space in between them. A pedestrian is standing on the median of the road facing a row house. The angle of elevation from the pedestrian to the top of the house is $30^\circ$. Find the width of the road.

---

## Procedure:

1. Draw a right-angled triangle using the house height and the distance from the pedestrian to the house.
2. Let the distance from the pedestrian to the house be $x$ m.
3. Apply the tangent ratio.
4. Find $x$.
5. Since the pedestrian is standing at the median, the width of the road is $2x$.

## Solution:

Let

$AB = 4\sqrt{3}$ m be the height of the house.

$BC = x$ m be the distance from the pedestrian to the row house.

$\angle C = 30^\circ$.

In right $\triangle ABC$,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\tan 30^\circ
&= \frac{AB}{BC}
\\[4pt]
\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}
&= \frac{4\sqrt{3}}{x}
\\[4pt]
x
&= 4\sqrt{3}\times\sqrt{3}
\\[4pt]
&= 4\times 3
\\[4pt]
&= 12
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
BC = 12\text{ m}
$$

Since the pedestrian is standing on the median of the road,

$$
\text{Width of the road}
= 2\times 12
= 24\text{ m}
$$

$$
\boxed{\text{Width of the road} = 24\text{ m}}
$$

Hence proved.
