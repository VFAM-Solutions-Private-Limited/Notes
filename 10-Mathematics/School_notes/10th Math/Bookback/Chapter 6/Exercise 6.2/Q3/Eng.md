# Exercise 6.2

## Question 3:

To a man standing outside his house, the angles of elevation of the top and bottom of a window are $60^\circ$ and $45^\circ$ respectively. If the height of the man is 180 cm and if he is 5 m away from the wall, what is the height of the window? $(\sqrt{3}=1.732)$

---

## Procedure:

1. Draw the wall, window and man.
2. Consider the man's eye level as the point of observation.
3. Use $\tan 45^\circ$ to find the height of the bottom of the window from the eye level.
4. Use $\tan 60^\circ$ to find the height of the top of the window from the eye level.
5. Find the difference between the two heights to obtain the height of the window.

## Solution:

Given,

Distance of the man from the wall

$$
BC = 5\text{ m}
$$

Height of the man

$$
BE = 180\text{ cm}=1.8\text{ m}
$$

Let

$D$ be the bottom of the window.

$A$ be the top of the window.

Height of the window

$$
AD = h
$$

From right $\triangle EDC$,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\tan45^\circ
&=\frac{DC}{EC}
\\[4pt]
1
&=\frac{DC}{5}
\\[4pt]
DC
&=5\text{ m}
\end{aligned}
$$

From right $\triangle EAC$,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\tan60^\circ
&=\frac{AC}{EC}
\\[4pt]
\sqrt3
&=\frac{AC}{5}
\\[4pt]
AC
&=5\sqrt3
\\[4pt]
&=5(1.732)
\\[4pt]
&=8.66\text{ m}
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\begin{aligned}
AD
&=AC-DC
\\[4pt]
&=8.66-5
\\[4pt]
&=3.66\text{ m}
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence,

$$
\boxed{\text{Height of the window}=3.66\text{ m}}
$$

Hence proved.
