# Exercise 6.2

## Question 4:

A statue 1.6 m tall stands on the top of a pedestal. From a point on the ground, the angle of elevation of the top of the statue is $60^\circ$ and from the same point the angle of elevation of the top of the pedestal is $40^\circ$. Find the height of the pedestal.

(Given: $\tan 40^\circ = 0.8391,\ \sqrt{3} = 1.732$)

---

## Procedure:

1. Let the height of the pedestal be $h$ m.
2. Let the distance from the observation point to the pedestal be $x$ m.
3. Use the angle of elevation $60^\circ$ to form the first equation.
4. Use the angle of elevation $40^\circ$ to form the second equation.
5. Solve the equations to find the height of the pedestal.

## Solution:

Let

$AB = h$ m be the height of the pedestal.

$BD = 1.6$ m be the height of the statue.

$AC = x$ m be the distance from the point of observation.

Then,

$$
AD = h + 1.6
$$

In right $\triangle ADC$,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\tan 60^\circ
&= \frac{AD}{AC}
\\[4pt]
1.732
&= \frac{h+1.6}{x}
\\[4pt]
h+1.6
&= 1.732x
\end{aligned}
$$

In right $\triangle ABC$,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\tan 40^\circ
&= \frac{AB}{AC}
\\[4pt]
0.8391
&= \frac{h}{x}
\\[4pt]
h
&= 0.8391x
\end{aligned}
$$

Substituting,

$$
\begin{aligned}
0.8391x + 1.6
&= 1.732x
\\[4pt]
1.6
&= 0.8929x
\\[4pt]
x
&= \frac{1.6}{0.8929}
\\[4pt]
&\approx 1.792
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\begin{aligned}
h
&= 0.8391(1.792)
\\[4pt]
&\approx 1.504
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence,

$$
\boxed{\text{Height of the pedestal} \approx 1.5\text{ m}}
$$

Hence proved.
