# Exercise 6.2

## Question 5:

A flag pole of height $h$ metres is on the top of the hemispherical dome of radius $r$ metres. A man is standing 7 m away from the dome. Seeing the top of the pole at an angle $45^\circ$ and moving 5 m away from the dome and seeing the bottom of the pole at an angle $30^\circ$. Find

(i) the height of the pole

(ii) radius of the dome.

$(\sqrt{3}=1.732)$

## Procedure:

1. Let the height of the pole be $h$ m.
2. Let the radius of the hemispherical dome be $r$ m.
3. Use the angle of elevation $45^\circ$ to form the first equation.
4. Use the angle of elevation $30^\circ$ to form the second equation.
5. Solve the equations simultaneously to find $r$ and $h$.

---

## Solution:

Let

Radius of the dome $= r$ m

Height of the pole $= h$ m

From the figure,

Distance of first observation point from the centre

$$
= r+7
$$

Distance of second observation point from the centre

$$
= r+12
$$

### Using angle of elevation $45^\circ$

$$
\begin{aligned}
\tan45^\circ
&=\frac{r+h}{r+7}
\\[4pt]
1
&=\frac{r+h}{r+7}
\\[4pt]
r+h
&=r+7
\\[4pt]
h
&=7
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{h=7\text{ m}}
$$

### Using angle of elevation $30^\circ$

$$
\begin{aligned}
\tan30^\circ
&=\frac{r}{r+12}
\\[4pt]
\frac{1}{\sqrt3}
&=\frac{r}{r+12}
\\[4pt]
r+12
&=\sqrt3\,r
\\[4pt]
12
&=(\sqrt3-1)r
\end{aligned}
$$

Substituting $\sqrt3=1.732$,

$$
\begin{aligned}
12
&=(1.732-1)r
\\[4pt]
12
&=0.732r
\\[4pt]
r
&=\frac{12}{0.732}
\\[4pt]
&\approx16.39
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{r=16.39\text{ m}}
$$

---

## Answer:

(i)

$$
\boxed{\text{Height of the pole}=7\text{ m}}
$$

(ii)

$$
\boxed{\text{Radius of the dome}=16.39\text{ m}}
$$

Hence proved.
