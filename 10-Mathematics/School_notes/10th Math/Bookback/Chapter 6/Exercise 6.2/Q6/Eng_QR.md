## Question 6:

The top of a 15 m high tower makes an angle of elevation of $60^\circ$ with the bottom of an electronic pole and an angle of elevation of $30^\circ$ with the top of the pole. What is the height of the electric pole?

## Solution:

Let

Height of tower

$$
AB = 15\text{ m}
$$

Height of electric pole

$$
CD = h\text{ m}
$$

Distance between the tower and pole

$$
BC = x\text{ m}
$$

### Using angle of elevation $60^\circ$

In right $\triangle ACB$,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\tan 60^\circ
&= \frac{AB}{BC}
\\[4pt]
\sqrt3
&= \frac{15}{x}
\\[4pt]
x
&= \frac{15}{\sqrt3}
\\[4pt]
&= 5\sqrt3
\end{aligned}
$$

### Using angle of elevation $30^\circ$

In right $\triangle ADE$,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\tan 30^\circ
&= \frac{h-15}{x}
\\[4pt]
\frac{1}{\sqrt3}
&= \frac{h-15}{5\sqrt3}
\\[4pt]
h-15
&= 5
\\[4pt]
h
&= 20
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\text{Height of the electric pole} = 20\text{ m}}
$$

Hence proved.
