# Exercise 6.3

## Question 1:

From the top of a rock $50\sqrt{3}$ m high, the angle of depression of a car on the ground is observed to be $30^\circ$. Find the distance of the car from the rock.

---

## Procedure:

1. Draw the right-angled triangle using the given height of the rock and the distance of the car from the rock.
2. Use the fact that the angle of depression is equal to the angle of elevation.
3. Apply the trigonometric ratio $\tan \theta$.
4. Substitute the given values and solve for the required distance.

## Solution:

Let

- $AB = 50\sqrt{3}$ m be the height of the rock.
- $BC = x$ m be the distance of the car from the rock.

Since the angle of depression is $30^\circ$,

$$
\angle C = 30^\circ
$$

In right $\triangle ABC$,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\tan 30^\circ
&= \frac{AB}{BC}
\\
\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}
&= \frac{50\sqrt{3}}{x}
\\
x
&= 50\sqrt{3}\times\sqrt{3}
\\
&= 50\times3
\\
&= 150
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore, the distance of the car from the rock is

$$
\boxed{150\ \text{m}}
$$
