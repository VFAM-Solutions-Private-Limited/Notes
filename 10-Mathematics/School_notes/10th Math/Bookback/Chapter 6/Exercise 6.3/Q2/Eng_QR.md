# Exercise 6.3

## Question 2:

The horizontal distance between two buildings is 70 m. The angle of depression of the top of the first building when seen from the top of the second building is $45^\circ$. If the height of the second building is 120 m, find the height of the first building.

## Solution:

Let

- $AB = x$ m be the height of the first building.
- $CD = 120$ m be the height of the second building.
- $BD = 70$ m be the horizontal distance between the buildings.

Since the angle of depression is $45^\circ$,

$$
\angle ACB = 45^\circ
$$

In right $\triangle ACE$,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\tan 45^\circ
&= \frac{CE}{AE}
\\
1
&= \frac{120-x}{70}
\\
120-x
&= 70
\\
x
&= 120-70
\\
x
&= 50
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore, the height of the first building is

$$
\boxed{50\ \text{m}}
$$
