# Exercise 6.3

## Question 3:

From the top of the tower 60 m high the angles of depression of the top and bottom of a vertical lamp post are observed to be $38^\circ$ and $60^\circ$ respectively. Find the height of the lamp post.

$(\tan 38^\circ = 0.7813,\ \sqrt{3}=1.732)$

## Solution:

Let

- $AB = 60$ m be the height of the tower.
- $CD = h$ m be the height of the lamp post.
- $BD = x$ m be the horizontal distance between the tower and the lamp post.

Since the angle of depression to the bottom of the lamp post is $60^\circ$,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\tan 60^\circ
&= \frac{AB}{BD}
\\
\sqrt{3}
&= \frac{60}{x}
\\
x
&= \frac{60}{\sqrt{3}}
\\
&= \frac{60}{1.732}
\\
&= 34.64 \text{ m}
\end{aligned}
$$

Now, the angle of depression to the top of the lamp post is $38^\circ$.

$$
\begin{aligned}
\tan 38^\circ
&= \frac{AB-CD}{BD}
\\
0.7813
&= \frac{60-h}{34.64}
\\
60-h
&= 34.64 \times 0.7813
\\
&= 27.06
\\
h
&= 60-27.06
\\
&= 32.94
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore, the height of the lamp post is

$$
\boxed{32.94\ \text{m}}
$$
