# Exercise 6.3

## Question 4:

An aeroplane at an altitude of 1800 m finds that two boats are sailing towards it in the same direction. The angles of depression of the boats as observed from the aeroplane are $60^\circ$ and $30^\circ$ respectively. Find the distance between the two boats.

$(\sqrt{3}=1.732)$

---

## Procedure:

1. Draw the aeroplane at a height of 1800 m above the sea level.
2. Let the distances of the two boats from the foot of the aeroplane be $x$ m and $y$ m.
3. Use the angle of depression $60^\circ$ to find the distance of the nearer boat.
4. Use the angle of depression $30^\circ$ to find the distance of the farther boat.
5. Find the difference between these distances to obtain the distance between the boats.

## Solution:

Let

- $AB = 1800$ m be the altitude of the aeroplane.
- $BC = x$ m be the distance of the nearer boat from the foot of the aeroplane.
- $BD = y$ m be the distance of the farther boat from the foot of the aeroplane.

Since the angles of depression are equal to the corresponding angles of elevation,

### For the nearer boat:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\tan 60^\circ
&= \frac{AB}{BC}
\\
1.732
&= \frac{1800}{x}
\\
x
&= \frac{1800}{1.732}
\\
&= 1039.26\ \text{m}
\end{aligned}
$$

### For the farther boat:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\tan 30^\circ
&= \frac{AB}{BD}
\\
\frac{1}{1.732}
&= \frac{1800}{y}
\\
y
&= 1800 \times 1.732
\\
&= 3117.60\ \text{m}
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore, the distance between the two boats is

$$
\begin{aligned}
CD
&= BD-BC
\\
&= 3117.60-1039.26
\\
&= 2078.34\text{ m}
\end{aligned}
$$

**Answer:** $\boxed{2078.34\text{ m}}$
