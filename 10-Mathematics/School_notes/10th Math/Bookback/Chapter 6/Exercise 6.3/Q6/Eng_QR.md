# Exercise 6.3

## Question 6:

A lift in a building of height 90 feet with transparent glass walls is descending from the top of the building. At the top of the building, the angle of depression to a fountain in the garden is $60^\circ$. Two minutes later, the angle of depression reduces to $30^\circ$. If the fountain is $30\sqrt{3}$ feet from the entrance of the lift, find the speed of the lift which is descending.

## Solution:

Let

- $AB = 90$ ft be the height of the building.
- $BC = 30\sqrt{3}$ ft be the horizontal distance from the building to the fountain.
- $D$ be the position of the lift after 2 minutes.

At the top of the building,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\tan 60^\circ
&= \frac{AB}{BC}
\\
\sqrt{3}
&= \frac{90}{30\sqrt{3}}
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence the given measurements are consistent.

After 2 minutes, the angle of depression becomes $30^\circ$.

In right $\triangle DBC$,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\tan 30^\circ
&= \frac{DB}{BC}
\\
\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}
&= \frac{DB}{30\sqrt{3}}
\\
DB
&= \frac{30\sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{3}}
\\
&= 30
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore, the height of the lift after 2 minutes is

$$
DB = 30\text{ ft}
$$

Distance descended by the lift

$$
\begin{aligned}
AD
&= AB - DB
\\
&= 90 - 30
\\
&= 60\text{ ft}
\end{aligned}
$$

Time taken

$$
2\text{ minutes}
$$

Hence, speed of the lift

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Speed}
&= \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Time}}
\\
&= \frac{60}{2}
\\
&= 30\text{ ft/min}
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{30\text{ ft/min}}
$$
