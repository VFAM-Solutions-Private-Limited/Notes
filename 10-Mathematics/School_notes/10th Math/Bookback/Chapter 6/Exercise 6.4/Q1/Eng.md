# Exercise 6.4

## Question 1:

From the top of a tree of height 13 m the angle of elevation and depression of the top and bottom of another tree are 45° and 30° respectively. Find the height of the second tree.

$(\sqrt{3}=1.732)$

## Procedure:

1. Let the height of the first tree be 13 m.
2. Let the height of the second tree be $h$ m.
3. Use the angle of depression $30^\circ$ to find the horizontal distance between the trees.
4. Use the angle of elevation $45^\circ$ to find the difference in heights of the two trees.
5. Calculate the height of the second tree.

---

## Solution:

Let

- $AB = 13$ m be the height of the first tree.
- $CD = h$ m be the height of the second tree.
- $BC = x$ m be the horizontal distance between the trees.

Since the angle of depression to the foot of the second tree is $30^\circ$,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\tan 30^\circ
&= \frac{AB}{BC}
\\
\frac{1}{1.732}
&= \frac{13}{x}
\\
x
&= 13 \times 1.732
\\
&= 22.516
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
BC = 22.516\text{ m}
$$

Since the angle of elevation to the top of the second tree is $45^\circ$,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\tan 45^\circ
&= \frac{CD-AB}{BC}
\\
1
&= \frac{h-13}{22.516}
\\
h-13
&= 22.516
\\
h
&= 35.516
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore, the height of the second tree is

$$
\boxed{35.516\text{ m}}
$$
