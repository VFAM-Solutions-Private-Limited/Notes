# Exercise 6.4

## Question 2:

A man is standing on the deck of a ship, which is 40 m above water level. He observes the angle of elevation of the top of a hill as 60° and the angle of depression of the base of the hill as 30°. Calculate the distance of the hill from the ship and the height of the hill.

$(\sqrt{3}=1.732)$

## Solution:

Let

- $AB = 40$ m be the height of the observer above water level.
- $BC = x$ m be the horizontal distance of the hill from the ship.
- $CD = h$ m be the height of the hill.

### Distance of the hill from the ship

Since the angle of depression of the base of the hill is $30^\circ$,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\tan 30^\circ
&= \frac{AB}{BC}
\\
\frac{1}{1.732}
&= \frac{40}{x}
\\
x
&= 40 \times 1.732
\\
&= 69.28
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
BC = 69.28\text{ m}
$$

### Height of the hill

Since the angle of elevation of the top of the hill is $60^\circ$,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\tan 60^\circ
&= \frac{CD-AB}{BC}
\\
1.732
&= \frac{h-40}{69.28}
\\
h-40
&= 69.28 \times 1.732
\\
&= 119.99
\\
h
&= 159.99
\end{aligned}
$$

$$
h \approx 160
$$

Therefore, the height of the hill is

$$
\boxed{160\text{ m}}
$$

---

### Answer

1. Distance of the hill from the ship:

$$
\boxed{69.28\text{ m}}
$$

2. Height of the hill:

$$
\boxed{160\text{ m}}
$$
