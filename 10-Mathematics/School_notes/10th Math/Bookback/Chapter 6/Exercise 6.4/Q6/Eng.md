# Exercise 6.4

## Question 6:

Three villagers A, B and C can see each other using telescope across a valley. The horizontal distance between A and B is 8 km and the horizontal distance between B and C is 12 km. The angle of depression of B from A is 20° and the angle of elevation of C from B is 30°. Calculate :

(i) the vertical height between A and B.

(ii) the vertical height between B and C.

$(\tan 20^\circ = 0.3640,\ \sqrt{3}=1.732)$

## Procedure:

1. Use the horizontal distance between A and B and the angle of depression $20^\circ$ to find the vertical height between A and B.
2. Use the horizontal distance between B and C and the angle of elevation $30^\circ$ to find the vertical height between B and C.
3. Apply the tangent ratio in each right triangle.
4. Calculate the required vertical heights.

---

## Solution:

### (i) Vertical height between A and B

Let the vertical height between A and B be $h_1$ km.

Given,

- Horizontal distance $AB = 8$ km
- Angle of depression of B from A $= 20^\circ$

$$
\begin{aligned}
\tan 20^\circ
&= \frac{h_1}{8}
\\
0.3640
&= \frac{h_1}{8}
\\
h_1
&= 8 \times 0.3640
\\
&= 2.912
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{h_1 = 2.912\text{ km}}
$$

---

### (ii) Vertical height between B and C

Let the vertical height between B and C be $h_2$ km.

Given,

- Horizontal distance $BC = 12$ km
- Angle of elevation of C from B $= 30^\circ$

$$
\begin{aligned}
\tan 30^\circ
&= \frac{h_2}{12}
\\
\frac{1}{1.732}
&= \frac{h_2}{12}
\\
h_2
&= \frac{12}{1.732}
\\
&= 6.93
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{h_2 = 6.93\text{ km}}
$$

---

### Answers

(i) Vertical height between A and B

$$
\boxed{2.912\text{ km}}
$$

(ii) Vertical height between B and C

$$
\boxed{6.93\text{ km}}
$$
