# Exercise 7.1

## Question 2:

A solid iron cylinder has total surface area of 1848 sq.cm. Its curved surface area is five – sixth of its total surface area. Find the radius and height of the iron cylinder.

---

## Procedure:

1. Find the curved surface area using the given ratio.
2. Apply the formula for curved surface area of a cylinder.
3. Apply the formula for total surface area of a cylinder.
4. Solve for the radius.
5. Find the height of the cylinder.

---

## Solution:

Given,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Total Surface Area (TSA)}
&= 1848\text{ cm}^2
\\
\text{Curved Surface Area (CSA)}
&= \frac{5}{6}\times \text{TSA}
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{CSA}
&= \frac{5}{6}\times1848
\\
&= 1540\text{ cm}^2
\end{aligned}
$$

For a cylinder,

$$
\text{CSA}=2\pi rh
$$

$$
\begin{aligned}
1540
&=2\times\frac{22}{7}\times r\times h
\\
1540
&=\frac{44}{7}rh
\\
rh
&=245
\end{aligned}
$$

Also,

$$
\text{TSA}=2\pi r(r+h)
$$

$$
\begin{aligned}
1848
&=2\times\frac{22}{7}\times r(r+h)
\\
1848
&=\frac{44}{7}r(r+h)
\\
r(r+h)
&=294
\end{aligned}
$$

Substituting \(rh=245\),

$$
\begin{aligned}
r^2+rh
&=294
\\
r^2+245
&=294
\\
r^2
&=49
\\
r
&=7\text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Now,

$$
\begin{aligned}
rh
&=245
\\
7h
&=245
\\
h
&=35\text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\text{Radius}=7\text{ cm}}
$$

$$
\boxed{\text{Height}=35\text{ cm}}
$$
