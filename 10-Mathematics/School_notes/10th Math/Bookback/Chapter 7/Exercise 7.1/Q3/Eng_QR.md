# Exercise 7.1

## Question 3:

The external radius and the length of a hollow wooden log are 16 cm and 13 cm respectively. If its thickness is 4 cm then find its T.S.A.

---
## Solution:

Given,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{External radius }(R)
&=16\text{ cm}
\\
\text{Length }(h)
&=13\text{ cm}
\\
\text{Thickness}
&=4\text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Internal radius,

$$
\begin{aligned}
r
&=R-\text{Thickness}
\\
&=16-4
\\
&=12\text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Total surface area of a hollow cylinder,

$$
\text{T.S.A.}
=
2\pi h(R+r)
+
2\pi(R^2-r^2)
$$

Substituting the values,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{T.S.A.}
&=
2\times\frac{22}{7}\times13\times(16+12)
+
2\times\frac{22}{7}\times(16^2-12^2)
\\
&=
2\times\frac{22}{7}\times13\times28
+
2\times\frac{22}{7}\times(256-144)
\\
&=
2288
+
704
\\
&=
2992\text{ cm}^2
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\text{T.S.A.}=2992\text{ cm}^2}
$$
