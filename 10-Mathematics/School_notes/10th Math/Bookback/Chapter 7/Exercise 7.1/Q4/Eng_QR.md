# Exercise 7.1

## Question 4:

A right angled triangle PQR where ∠Q = 90° is rotated about QR and PQ. If QR = 16 cm and PR = 20 cm, compare the curved surface areas of the right circular cones so formed by the triangle.

---

## Procedure:

1. Find the third side of the right angled triangle using Pythagoras theorem.
2. Form the first cone by rotating the triangle about QR and find its curved surface area.
3. Form the second cone by rotating the triangle about PQ and find its curved surface area.
4. Compare the curved surface areas.

---

## Solution:

Given,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\angle Q &= 90^\circ
\\
QR &= 16\text{ cm}
\\
PR &= 20\text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Using Pythagoras theorem,

$$
\begin{aligned}
PR^2
&= PQ^2 + QR^2
\\
20^2
&= PQ^2 + 16^2
\\
400
&= PQ^2 + 256
\\
PQ^2
&= 144
\\
PQ
&= 12\text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

### Cone formed by rotating about QR

When the triangle is rotated about QR,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Radius }(r_1)
&= PQ
\\
&= 12\text{ cm}
\\
\text{Slant height }(l_1)
&= PR
\\
&= 20\text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Curved surface area,

$$
\begin{aligned}
CSA_1
&= \pi r_1 l_1
\\
&= \pi \times 12 \times 20
\\
&= 240\pi
\end{aligned}
$$

### Cone formed by rotating about PQ

When the triangle is rotated about PQ,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Radius }(r_2)
&= QR
\\
&= 16\text{ cm}
\\
\text{Slant height }(l_2)
&= PR
\\
&= 20\text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Curved surface area,

$$
\begin{aligned}
CSA_2
&= \pi r_2 l_2
\\
&= \pi \times 16 \times 20
\\
&= 320\pi
\end{aligned}
$$

### Comparison

$$
\begin{aligned}
CSA_1 : CSA_2
&= 240\pi : 320\pi
\\
&= 3 : 4
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{CSA_1 : CSA_2 = 3 : 4}
$$
