# Exercise 7.1

## Question 6:

A girl wishes to prepare birthday caps in the form of right circular cones for her birthday party, using a sheet of paper whose area is 5720 cm², how many caps can be made with radius 5 cm and height 12 cm.

---

## Solution:

Given,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Area of the sheet}
&= 5720\text{ cm}^2
\\
\text{Radius of the cap }(r)
&= 5\text{ cm}
\\
\text{Height of the cap }(h)
&= 12\text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Slant height of the cone,

$$
\begin{aligned}
l
&= \sqrt{r^2+h^2}
\\
&= \sqrt{5^2+12^2}
\\
&= \sqrt{25+144}
\\
&= \sqrt{169}
\\
&= 13\text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Curved surface area of one cap,

$$
\begin{aligned}
CSA
&= \pi rl
\\
&= \frac{22}{7}\times5\times13
\\
&= \frac{1430}{7}
\\
&= 204.29\text{ cm}^2
\end{aligned}
$$

Number of caps that can be made,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Number of caps}
&= \frac{5720}{204.29}
\\
&\approx 28
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\text{Number of caps}=28}
$$
