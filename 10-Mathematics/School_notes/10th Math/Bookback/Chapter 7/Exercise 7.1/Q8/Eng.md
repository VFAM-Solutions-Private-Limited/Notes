# Exercise 7.1

## Question 8:

The radius of a sphere increases by 25%. Find the percentage increase in its surface area.

---

## Procedure:

1. Let the original radius of the sphere be \(r\).
2. Find the new radius after the increase.
3. Calculate the original surface area.
4. Calculate the new surface area.
5. Find the percentage increase in surface area.

---

## Solution:

Given,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Increase in radius}
&= 25\%
\end{aligned}
$$

Let the original radius of the sphere be

$$
r
$$

Then the new radius is

$$
\begin{aligned}
r'
&= r+\frac{25}{100}r
\\
&= \frac{5r}{4}
\end{aligned}
$$

Original surface area of the sphere,

$$
\begin{aligned}
S_1
&= 4\pi r^2
\end{aligned}
$$

New surface area of the sphere,

$$
\begin{aligned}
S_2
&= 4\pi\left(\frac{5r}{4}\right)^2
\\
&= 4\pi\times\frac{25r^2}{16}
\\
&= \frac{25}{4}\pi r^2
\end{aligned}
$$

Ratio of the surface areas,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{S_2}{S_1}
&=
\frac{\frac{25}{4}\pi r^2}{4\pi r^2}
\\
&=
\frac{25}{16}
\end{aligned}
$$

Percentage increase,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Percentage increase}
&=
\frac{S_2-S_1}{S_1}\times100
\\
&=
\left(\frac{25}{16}-1\right)\times100
\\
&=
\frac{9}{16}\times100
\\
&=
56.25\%
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\text{Percentage increase in surface area}=56.25\%}
$$
