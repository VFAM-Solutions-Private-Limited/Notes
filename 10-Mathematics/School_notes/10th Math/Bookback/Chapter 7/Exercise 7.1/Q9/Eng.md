# Exercise 7.1

## Question 9:

The internal and external diameters of a hollow hemispherical vessel are 20 cm and 28 cm respectively. Find the cost to paint the vessel all over at ₹ 0.14 per cm².

---

## Procedure:

1. Find the internal and external radii of the hemispherical vessel.
2. Calculate the total surface area of the hollow hemisphere.
3. Find the area to be painted.
4. Multiply the painted area by the cost per cm².
5. Find the total cost.

---

## Solution:

Given,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Internal diameter}
&=20\text{ cm}
\\
\text{External diameter}
&=28\text{ cm}
\\
\text{Cost of painting}
&=₹0.14\text{ per cm}^2
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Internal radius }(r)
&=\frac{20}{2}
\\
&=10\text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{External radius }(R)
&=\frac{28}{2}
\\
&=14\text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Since the vessel is painted all over,

$$
\text{Area to be painted}
=
2\pi R^2
+
2\pi r^2
+
\pi(R^2-r^2)
$$

Substituting the values,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Area}
&=
2\times\frac{22}{7}\times14^2
+
2\times\frac{22}{7}\times10^2
+
\frac{22}{7}(14^2-10^2)
\\
&=
1232
+
628.57
+
301.71
\\
&=
2162.28\text{ cm}^2
\end{aligned}
$$

Cost of painting,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Cost}
&=
2162.28\times0.14
\\
&=
302.72
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\text{Cost of painting}=₹302.72}
$$
