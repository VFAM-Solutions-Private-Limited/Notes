# Exercise 7.1

## Question 9:

The internal and external diameters of a hollow hemispherical vessel are 20 cm and 28 cm respectively. Find the cost to paint the vessel all over at ₹ 0.14 per cm².

---
## Solution:

Given,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Internal diameter}
&=20\text{ cm}
\\
\text{External diameter}
&=28\text{ cm}
\\
\text{Cost of painting}
&=₹0.14\text{ per cm}^2
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Internal radius }(r)
&=\frac{20}{2}
\\
&=10\text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{External radius }(R)
&=\frac{28}{2}
\\
&=14\text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Since the vessel is painted all over,

$$
\text{Area to be painted}
=
2\pi R^2
+
2\pi r^2
+
\pi(R^2-r^2)
$$

Substituting the values,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Area}
&=
2\times\frac{22}{7}\times14^2
+
2\times\frac{22}{7}\times10^2
+
\frac{22}{7}(14^2-10^2)
\\
&=
1232
+
628.57
+
301.71
\\
&=
2162.28\text{ cm}^2
\end{aligned}
$$

Cost of painting,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Cost}
&=
2162.28\times0.14
\\
&=
302.72
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\text{Cost of painting}=₹302.72}
$$
