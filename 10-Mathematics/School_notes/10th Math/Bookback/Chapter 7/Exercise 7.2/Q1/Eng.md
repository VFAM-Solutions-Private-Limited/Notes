# Exercise 7.2

## Question 1:

A 14 m deep well with inner diameter 10 m is dug and the earth taken out is evenly spread all around the well to form an embankment of width 5 m. Find the height of the embankment.

---

## Procedure:

1. Find the volume of earth dug out from the well.
2. Find the outer radius of the embankment.
3. Find the volume of the embankment formed.
4. Equate the two volumes.
5. Calculate the height of the embankment.

---

## Solution:

Given,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Depth of the well}
&= 14 \text{ m}
\\
\text{Inner diameter of the well}
&= 10 \text{ m}
\\
\text{Radius of the well }(r)
&= \frac{10}{2}
\\
&= 5 \text{ m}
\\
\text{Width of the embankment}
&= 5 \text{ m}
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of earth dug out from the well,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_1
&= \pi r^2 h
\\
&= \pi \times 5^2 \times 14
\\
&= 350\pi \text{ m}^3
\end{aligned}
$$

The embankment forms a hollow cylinder.

Outer radius of the embankment,

$$
\begin{aligned}
R
&= 5 + 5
\\
&= 10 \text{ m}
\end{aligned}
$$

Let the height of the embankment be \(H\) m.

Volume of the embankment,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_2
&= \pi (R^2-r^2)H
\\
&= \pi (10^2-5^2)H
\\
&= \pi (100-25)H
\\
&= 75\pi H
\end{aligned}
$$

Since the earth dug out is used to form the embankment,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_1
&= V_2
\\
350\pi
&= 75\pi H
\\
350
&= 75H
\\
H
&= \frac{350}{75}
\\
&= \frac{14}{3}
\\
&= 4.67
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\text{Height of the embankment}=4.67 \text{ m}}
$$
