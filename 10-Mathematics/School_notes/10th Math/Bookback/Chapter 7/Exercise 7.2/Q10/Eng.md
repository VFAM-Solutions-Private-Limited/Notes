# Exercise 7.2

## Question 10:

A container open at the top is in the form of a frustum of a cone of height 16 cm with radii of its lower and upper ends are 8 cm and 20 cm respectively. Find the cost of milk which can completely fill a container at the rate of ₹40 per litre.

---

## Procedure:

1. Identify the dimensions of the frustum.
2. Apply the volume formula of a frustum.
3. Calculate the volume of the container.
4. Convert the volume into litres.
5. Find the cost at ₹40 per litre.

---

## Solution:

Given,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Height }(h)
&= 16 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{Radius of lower end }(r)
&= 8 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{Radius of upper end }(R)
&= 20 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{Cost of milk}
&= ₹40 \text{ per litre}
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of a frustum,

$$
V
=
\frac{1}{3}\pi h(R^2+r^2+Rr)
$$

Substituting the values,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V
&=
\frac{1}{3}\times\frac{22}{7}\times16
\left(20^2+8^2+20\times8\right)
\\
&=
\frac{22}{21}\times16(400+64+160)
\\
&=
\frac{22}{21}\times16\times624
\\
&=
20937.14 \text{ cm}^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Since,

$$
1000 \text{ cm}^3 = 1 \text{ litre}
$$

Volume in litres,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V
&=
\frac{20937.14}{1000}
\\
&=
20.937 \text{ litres}
\end{aligned}
$$

Cost of milk,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Cost}
&=
20.937\times40
\\
&=
837.49
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\text{Cost of milk}=₹837.49}
$$

or approximately,

$$
\boxed{\text{Cost of milk}=₹837.50}
$$
