# Exercise 7.2

## Question 2:

A cylindrical glass with diameter 20 cm has water to a height of 9 cm. A small cylindrical metal of radius 5 cm and height 4 cm is immersed completely. Calculate the raise of the water in the glass?

---

## Solution:

Given,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Diameter of the glass}
&= 20 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{Radius of the glass }(R)
&= \frac{20}{2}
\\
&= 10 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{Radius of the metal cylinder }(r)
&= 5 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{Height of the metal cylinder}
&= 4 \text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of the metal cylinder,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V
&= \pi r^2 h
\\
&= \pi \times 5^2 \times 4
\\
&= 100\pi \text{ cm}^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Let the rise in the water level be \(x\) cm.

Volume of water displaced,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V
&= \pi R^2 x
\\
&= \pi \times 10^2 \times x
\\
&= 100\pi x
\end{aligned}
$$

Since the volume of displaced water equals the volume of the metal cylinder,

$$
\begin{aligned}
100\pi x
&= 100\pi
\\
x
&= 1
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\text{Rise in water level}=1 \text{ cm}}
$$
