# Exercise 7.2

## Question 3:

If the circumference of a conical wooden piece is 484 cm then find its volume when its height is 105 cm.

---

## Procedure:

1. Find the radius of the base using the circumference.
2. Use the volume formula of a cone.
3. Substitute the values.
4. Calculate the volume.

---

## Solution:

Given,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Circumference of the base}
&= 484 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{Height }(h)
&= 105 \text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Circumference of a circle,

$$
2\pi r = 484
$$

$$
\begin{aligned}
2\times\frac{22}{7}\times r
&= 484
\\
\frac{44}{7}r
&= 484
\\
r
&= \frac{484\times7}{44}
\\
&= 77 \text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of the cone,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V
&= \frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h
\\
&= \frac{1}{3}\times\frac{22}{7}\times77^2\times105
\\
&= \frac{22}{7}\times77^2\times35
\\
&= 652190 \text{ cm}^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\text{Volume of the cone}=652190 \text{ cm}^3}
$$
