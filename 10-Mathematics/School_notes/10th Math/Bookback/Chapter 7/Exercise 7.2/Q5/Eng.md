# Exercise 7.2

## Question 5:

A right angled triangle whose sides are 6 cm, 8 cm and 10 cm is revolved about the sides containing the right angle in two ways. Find the difference in volumes of the two solids so formed.

---

## Procedure:

1. Form the first cone by revolving the triangle about the side of length 6 cm.
2. Find the volume of the first cone.
3. Form the second cone by revolving the triangle about the side of length 8 cm.
4. Find the volume of the second cone.
5. Calculate the difference between the two volumes.

---

## Solution:

Given,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Sides of the right angled triangle}
&= 6 \text{ cm},\; 8 \text{ cm},\; 10 \text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

### Cone formed by revolving about the side 6 cm

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Radius }(r_1)
&= 8 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{Height }(h_1)
&= 6 \text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of the cone,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_1
&= \frac{1}{3}\pi r_1^2 h_1
\\
&= \frac{1}{3}\pi(8)^2(6)
\\
&= 128\pi \text{ cm}^3
\end{aligned}
$$

### Cone formed by revolving about the side 8 cm

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Radius }(r_2)
&= 6 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{Height }(h_2)
&= 8 \text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of the cone,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_2
&= \frac{1}{3}\pi r_2^2 h_2
\\
&= \frac{1}{3}\pi(6)^2(8)
\\
&= 96\pi \text{ cm}^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Difference in volumes,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_1-V_2
&= 128\pi-96\pi
\\
&= 32\pi
\end{aligned}
$$

Substituting,

$$
\begin{aligned}
32\pi
&= 32\times\frac{22}{7}
\\
&= \frac{704}{7}
\\
&= 100.57
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\text{Difference in volumes}=32\pi \text{ cm}^3}
$$

or

$$
\boxed{\text{Difference in volumes}=100.57 \text{ cm}^3}
$$
