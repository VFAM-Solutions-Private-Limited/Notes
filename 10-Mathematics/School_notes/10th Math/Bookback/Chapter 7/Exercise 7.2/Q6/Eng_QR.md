# Exercise 7.2

## Question 6:

The volumes of two cones of same base radius are 3600 cm³ and 5040 cm³. Find the ratio of heights.

---

## Solution:

Given,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Volume of first cone }(V_1)
&= 3600 \text{ cm}^3
\\
\text{Volume of second cone }(V_2)
&= 5040 \text{ cm}^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Let the heights of the cones be \(h_1\) and \(h_2\).

Volume of a cone,

$$
V=\frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h
$$

Since the radii of the two cones are equal,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{V_1}{V_2}
&=
\frac{\frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h_1}
{\frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h_2}
\\
&=
\frac{h_1}{h_2}
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{h_1}{h_2}
&=
\frac{3600}{5040}
\\
&=
\frac{5}{7}
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence,

$$
\boxed{h_1:h_2=5:7}
$$
