# Exercise 7.2

## Question 7:

If the ratio of radii of two spheres is 4:7, find the ratio of their volumes.

---
## Solution:

Given
$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Ratio of radii}
&= 4:7
\end{aligned}
$$

Let the radii of the two spheres be

$$
\begin{aligned}
r_1
&= 4x
\\
r_2
&= 7x
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of a sphere,

$$
V=\frac{4}{3}\pi r^3
$$

Volume of the first sphere,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_1
&=\frac{4}{3}\pi(4x)^3
\\
&=\frac{4}{3}\pi(64x^3)
\\
&=\frac{256}{3}\pi x^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of the second sphere,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_2
&=\frac{4}{3}\pi(7x)^3
\\
&=\frac{4}{3}\pi(343x^3)
\\
&=\frac{1372}{3}\pi x^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_1:V_2
&=
\frac{256}{3}\pi x^3
:
\frac{1372}{3}\pi x^3
\\
&=
256:1372
\\
&=
64:343
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence,

$$
\boxed{V_1:V_2=64:343}
$$
