# Exercise 7.2

## Question 8:

A solid sphere and a solid hemisphere have equal total surface area. Prove that the ratio of their volume is 3√3 : 4.

---
## Solution:

Given,

A solid sphere and a solid hemisphere have equal total surface area.

Let

$$
\begin{aligned}
R
&= \text{radius of the sphere}
\\
r
&= \text{radius of the hemisphere}
\end{aligned}
$$

Total surface area of the sphere,

$$
4\pi R^2
$$

Total surface area of the hemisphere,

$$
3\pi r^2
$$

Since the total surface areas are equal,

$$
\begin{aligned}
4\pi R^2
&= 3\pi r^2
\\
4R^2
&= 3r^2
\\
R^2
&= \frac{3}{4}r^2
\\
R
&= \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}r
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of the sphere,

$$
V_1
=
\frac{4}{3}\pi R^3
$$

Volume of the hemisphere,

$$
V_2
=
\frac{2}{3}\pi r^3
$$

Therefore,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_1 : V_2
&=
\frac{4}{3}\pi R^3
:
\frac{2}{3}\pi r^3
\\
&=
2R^3 : r^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Substituting

$$
R=\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}r
$$

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_1 : V_2
&=
2\left(\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}r\right)^3 : r^3
\\
&=
2\left(\frac{3\sqrt{3}}{8}r^3\right) : r^3
\\
&=
\frac{3\sqrt{3}}{4}r^3 : r^3
\\
&=
3\sqrt{3} : 4
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence,

$$
\boxed{V_1 : V_2 = 3\sqrt{3} : 4}
$$
