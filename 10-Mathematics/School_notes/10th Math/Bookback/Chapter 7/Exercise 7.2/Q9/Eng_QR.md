# Exercise 7.2

## Question 9:

The outer and the inner surface areas of a spherical copper shell are 576π cm² and 324π cm² respectively. Find the volume of the material required to make the shell.

---
## Solution:

Given,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Outer surface area}
&= 576\pi \text{ cm}^2
\\
\text{Inner surface area}
&= 324\pi \text{ cm}^2
\end{aligned}
$$

Let \(R\) be the outer radius and \(r\) be the inner radius.

Outer surface area of a sphere,

$$
4\pi R^2=576\pi
$$

$$
\begin{aligned}
4R^2
&=576
\\
R^2
&=144
\\
R
&=12 \text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Inner surface area of a sphere,

$$
4\pi r^2=324\pi
$$

$$
\begin{aligned}
4r^2
&=324
\\
r^2
&=81
\\
r
&=9 \text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of material in the shell,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V
&=
\frac{4}{3}\pi(R^3-r^3)
\\
&=
\frac{4}{3}\pi(12^3-9^3)
\\
&=
\frac{4}{3}\pi(1728-729)
\\
&=
\frac{4}{3}\pi(999)
\\
&=
1332\pi
\end{aligned}
$$

Substituting \(\pi=\frac{22}{7}\),

$$
\begin{aligned}
V
&=
1332\times\frac{22}{7}
\\
&=
4186.29 \text{ cm}^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\text{Volume of the material}=1332\pi \text{ cm}^3}
$$

or

$$
\boxed{\text{Volume of the material}=4186.29 \text{ cm}^3}
$$
