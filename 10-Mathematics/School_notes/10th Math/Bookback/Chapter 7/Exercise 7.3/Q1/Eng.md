# Exercise 7.3

## Question 1:

A vessel is in the form of a hemispherical bowl mounted by a hollow cylinder. The diameter is 14 cm and the height of the vessel is 13 cm. Find the capacity of the vessel.

---

## Procedure:

1. Find the radius of the hemispherical bowl and cylinder.
2. Find the height of the cylindrical portion.
3. Calculate the volume of the hemisphere.
4. Calculate the volume of the cylinder.
5. Add the two volumes to find the capacity of the vessel.

---

## Solution:

Given,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Diameter of the vessel}
&= 14 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{Radius }(r)
&= \frac{14}{2}
\\
&= 7 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{Total height of the vessel}
&= 13 \text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Height of the cylindrical portion,

$$
\begin{aligned}
h
&= 13 - 7
\\
&= 6 \text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of the hemisphere,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_1
&= \frac{2}{3}\pi r^3
\\
&= \frac{2}{3}\times\frac{22}{7}\times7^3
\\
&= \frac{2156}{3}
\\
&= 718.67 \text{ cm}^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of the cylinder,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_2
&= \pi r^2 h
\\
&= \frac{22}{7}\times7^2\times6
\\
&= 924 \text{ cm}^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Capacity of the vessel,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V
&= V_1 + V_2
\\
&= 718.67 + 924
\\
&= 1642.67 \text{ cm}^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\text{Capacity of the vessel}=1642.67 \text{ cm}^3}
$$
