# Exercise 7.3

## Question 2:

Nathan, an engineering student was asked to make a model shaped like a cylinder with two cones attached at its two ends. The diameter of the model is 3 cm and its length is 12 cm. If each cone has a height of 2 cm, find the volume of the model that Nathan made.

---
## Solution:

Given,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Diameter of the model}
&= 3 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{Radius }(r)
&= \frac{3}{2}
\\
&= 1.5 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{Total length of the model}
&= 12 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{Height of each cone}
&= 2 \text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Length of the cylindrical portion,

$$
\begin{aligned}
h
&= 12 - 2 - 2
\\
&= 8 \text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of the cylinder,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_1
&= \pi r^2 h
\\
&= \frac{22}{7}\times(1.5)^2\times8
\\
&= \frac{396}{7}
\\
&= 56.57 \text{ cm}^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of one cone,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_2
&= \frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h
\\
&= \frac{1}{3}\times\frac{22}{7}\times(1.5)^2\times2
\\
&= \frac{33}{7}
\\
&= 4.71 \text{ cm}^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of two cones,

$$
\begin{aligned}
2V_2
&= 2\times4.71
\\
&= 9.43 \text{ cm}^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Total volume of the model,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V
&= V_1 + 2V_2
\\
&= 56.57 + 9.43
\\
&= 66 \text{ cm}^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\text{Volume of the model}=66 \text{ cm}^3}
$$
