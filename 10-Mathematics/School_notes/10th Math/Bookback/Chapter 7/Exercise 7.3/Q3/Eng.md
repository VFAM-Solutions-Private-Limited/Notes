# Exercise 7.3

## Question 3:

From a solid cylinder whose height is 2.4 cm and the diameter 1.4 cm, a cone of the same height and same diameter is carved out. Find the volume of the remaining solid to the nearest cm³.

---

## Procedure:

1. Find the radius of the cylinder.
2. Calculate the volume of the cylinder.
3. Calculate the volume of the cone having the same radius and height.
4. Subtract the volume of the cone from the volume of the cylinder.
5. Round off the answer to the nearest cm³.

---

## Solution:

Given,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Height}
&= 2.4 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{Diameter}
&= 1.4 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{Radius }(r)
&= \frac{1.4}{2}
\\
&= 0.7 \text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of the cylinder,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_1
&= \pi r^2 h
\\
&= \frac{22}{7}\times(0.7)^2\times2.4
\\
&= \frac{22}{7}\times0.49\times2.4
\\
&= 3.696 \text{ cm}^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of the cone,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_2
&= \frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h
\\
&= \frac{1}{3}\times\frac{22}{7}\times(0.7)^2\times2.4
\\
&= 1.232 \text{ cm}^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of the remaining solid,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V
&= V_1 - V_2
\\
&= 3.696 - 1.232
\\
&= 2.464 \text{ cm}^3
\end{aligned}
$$

To the nearest cubic centimetre,

$$
V \approx 2 \text{ cm}^3
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\text{Volume of the remaining solid}=2 \text{ cm}^3}
$$
