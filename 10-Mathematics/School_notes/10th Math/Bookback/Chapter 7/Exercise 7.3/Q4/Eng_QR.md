# Exercise 7.3

## Question 4:

A solid consisting of a right circular cone of height 12 cm and radius 6 cm standing on a hemisphere of radius 6 cm is placed upright in a right circular cylinder full of water such that it touches the bottom. Find the volume of the water displaced out of the cylinder, if the radius of the cylinder is 6 cm and height is 18 cm.

## Solution:

Given,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Radius of cone}
&= 6 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{Height of cone}
&= 12 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{Radius of hemisphere}
&= 6 \text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of the cone,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_1
&= \frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h
\\
&= \frac{1}{3}\times\frac{22}{7}\times6^2\times12
\\
&= 144\pi
\\
&= 452.57 \text{ cm}^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of the hemisphere,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_2
&= \frac{2}{3}\pi r^3
\\
&= \frac{2}{3}\times\frac{22}{7}\times6^3
\\
&= 144\pi
\\
&= 452.57 \text{ cm}^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of the solid,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V
&= V_1 + V_2
\\
&= 144\pi + 144\pi
\\
&= 288\pi
\\
&= 905.14 \text{ cm}^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Since the cylinder is completely filled with water, the volume of water displaced is equal to the volume of the solid immersed.

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\text{Volume of water displaced}=288\pi \text{ cm}^3}
$$

or

$$
\boxed{\text{Volume of water displaced}=905.14 \text{ cm}^3}
$$
