# Exercise 7.3

## Question 5:

A capsule is in the shape of a cylinder with two hemisphere stuck to each of its ends. If the length of the entire capsule is 12 mm and the diameter of the capsule is 3 mm, how much medicine it can hold?

---

## Procedure:

1. Find the radius of the capsule.
2. Find the length of the cylindrical portion.
3. Calculate the volume of the cylindrical portion.
4. Calculate the volume of the two hemispheres (i.e., one sphere).
5. Add the volumes to find the capacity of the capsule.

---

## Solution:

Given,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Length of the capsule}
&= 12 \text{ mm}
\\
\text{Diameter of the capsule}
&= 3 \text{ mm}
\\
\text{Radius }(r)
&= \frac{3}{2}
\\
&= 1.5 \text{ mm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Length of the cylindrical portion,

$$
\begin{aligned}
h
&= 12 - 2r
\\
&= 12 - 3
\\
&= 9 \text{ mm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of the cylindrical portion,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_1
&= \pi r^2 h
\\
&= \frac{22}{7}\times(1.5)^2\times9
\\
&= \frac{891}{14}
\\
&= 63.64 \text{ mm}^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of the two hemispheres,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_2
&= \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3
\\
&= \frac{4}{3}\times\frac{22}{7}\times(1.5)^3
\\
&= \frac{99}{7}
\\
&= 14.14 \text{ mm}^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of the capsule,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V
&= V_1 + V_2
\\
&= 63.64 + 14.14
\\
&= 77.78 \text{ mm}^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\text{Medicine the capsule can hold}=77.78 \text{ mm}^3}
$$
