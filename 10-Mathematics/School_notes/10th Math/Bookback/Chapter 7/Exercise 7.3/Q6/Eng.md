## Question 6:

As shown in figure a cubical block of side 7 cm is surmounted by a hemisphere. Find the surface area of the solid.

## Procedure:

1. Find the surface area of the cube.
2. Find the radius of the hemisphere.
3. Find the curved surface area of the hemisphere.
4. Subtract the circular area covered by the hemisphere on the cube.
5. Add the remaining surface area of the cube and the curved surface area of the hemisphere.

---

## Solution:

Given,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Side of the cube}
&= 7 \text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Radius of the hemisphere,

$$
\begin{aligned}
r
&= \frac{7}{2}
\\
&= 3.5 \text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Surface area of the cube,

$$
\begin{aligned}
6a^2
&= 6(7)^2
\\
&= 294 \text{ cm}^2
\end{aligned}
$$

Curved surface area of the hemisphere,

$$
\begin{aligned}
2\pi r^2
&=
2\times\frac{22}{7}\times(3.5)^2
\\
&=
77 \text{ cm}^2
\end{aligned}
$$

Area of the circular portion covered by the hemisphere,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\pi r^2
&=
\frac{22}{7}\times(3.5)^2
\\
&=
38.5 \text{ cm}^2
\end{aligned}
$$

Surface area of the solid,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Surface Area}
&=
294-38.5+77
\\
&=
332.5 \text{ cm}^2
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\text{Surface area of the solid}=332.5 \text{ cm}^2}
$$
