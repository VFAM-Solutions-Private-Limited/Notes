# Exercise 7.3

## Question 7:

A right circular cylinder just enclose a sphere of radius r units. Calculate

(i) the surface area of the sphere

(ii) the curved surface area of the cylinder

(iii) the ratio of the areas obtained in (i) and (ii).

---
## Solution:

Given,

A sphere of radius \(r\) units is just enclosed by a right circular cylinder.

Therefore,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Radius of cylinder}
&= r
\\
\text{Height of cylinder}
&= 2r
\end{aligned}
$$

### (i) Surface area of the sphere

Surface area of a sphere,

$$
\begin{aligned}
A_1
&= 4\pi r^2
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\text{Surface area of the sphere}=4\pi r^2}
$$

### (ii) Curved surface area of the cylinder

Curved surface area of a cylinder,

$$
\begin{aligned}
A_2
&= 2\pi rh
\\
&= 2\pi r(2r)
\\
&= 4\pi r^2
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\text{Curved surface area of the cylinder}=4\pi r^2}
$$

### (iii) Ratio of the areas obtained in (i) and (ii)

$$
\begin{aligned}
A_1:A_2
&=
4\pi r^2 : 4\pi r^2
\\
&=
1:1
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\text{Ratio of the areas}=1:1}
$$
