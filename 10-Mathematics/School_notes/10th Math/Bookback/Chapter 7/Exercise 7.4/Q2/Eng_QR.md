# Exercise 7.4

## Question 2:

Water is flowing at the rate of 15 km per hour through a pipe of diameter 14 cm into a rectangular tank which is 50 m long and 44 m wide. Find the time in which the level of water in the tanks will rise by 21 cm.

---
## Solution:

Given,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Diameter of the pipe}
&= 14 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{Radius of the pipe }(r)
&= 7 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{Speed of water}
&= 15 \text{ km/hr}
\\
&= 1500000 \text{ cm/hr}
\\
\text{Length of tank}
&= 50 \text{ m}
\\
&= 5000 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{Width of tank}
&= 44 \text{ m}
\\
&= 4400 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{Rise in water level}
&= 21 \text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of water flowing through the pipe in one hour,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_1
&= \pi r^2 h
\\
&= \frac{22}{7}\times7^2\times1500000
\\
&= 231000000 \text{ cm}^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of water required to raise the water level by 21 cm,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_2
&= l\times b\times h
\\
&= 5000\times4400\times21
\\
&= 462000000 \text{ cm}^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Let the required time be \(t\) hours.

$$
\begin{aligned}
231000000 \times t
&= 462000000
\\
t
&= \frac{462000000}{231000000}
\\
&= 2
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\text{Time required}=2 \text{ hours}}
$$
