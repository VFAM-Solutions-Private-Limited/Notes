# Exercise 7.4

## Question 3:

A conical flask is full of water. The flask has base radius r units and height h units, the water is poured into a cylindrical flask of base radius xr units. Find the height of water in the cylindrical flask.

---
## Solution:

Given,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Radius of the conical flask}
&= r
\\
\text{Height of the conical flask}
&= h
\\
\text{Radius of the cylindrical flask}
&= xr
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of water in the conical flask,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_1
&= \frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h
\end{aligned}
$$

Let the height of water in the cylindrical flask be \(H\).

Volume of water in the cylindrical flask,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_2
&= \pi (xr)^2 H
\\
&= \pi x^2 r^2 H
\end{aligned}
$$

Since the water from the conical flask is completely poured into the cylindrical flask,

$$
V_1 = V_2
$$

$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h
&= \pi x^2 r^2 H
\\
\frac{1}{3}h
&= x^2 H
\\
H
&= \frac{h}{3x^2}
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\text{Height of water in the cylindrical flask}=\frac{h}{3x^2}}
$$
