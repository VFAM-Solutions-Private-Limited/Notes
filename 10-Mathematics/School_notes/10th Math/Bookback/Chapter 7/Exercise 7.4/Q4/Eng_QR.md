# Exercise 7.4

## Question 4:

A solid right circular cone of diameter 14 cm and height 8 cm is melted to form a hollow sphere. If the external diameter of the sphere is 10 cm, find the internal diameter.

---
## Solution:

Given,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Diameter of cone}
&= 14 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{Radius of cone }(R)
&= 7 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{Height of cone}
&= 8 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{External diameter of sphere}
&= 10 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{External radius of sphere }(r_1)
&= 5 \text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of the cone,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V
&=
\frac{1}{3}\pi R^2 h
\\
&=
\frac{1}{3}\pi(7)^2(8)
\\
&=
\frac{392}{3}\pi
\end{aligned}
$$

Let \(r\) cm be the internal radius of the hollow sphere.

Volume of material in the hollow sphere,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V
&=
\frac{4}{3}\pi(r_1^3-r^3)
\\
&=
\frac{4}{3}\pi(5^3-r^3)
\end{aligned}
$$

Since the cone is melted to form the hollow sphere,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{392}{3}\pi
&=
\frac{4}{3}\pi(125-r^3)
\\
392
&=
4(125-r^3)
\\
98
&=
125-r^3
\\
r^3
&=
27
\\
r
&=
3 \text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Internal diameter}
&= 2r
\\
&= 2(3)
\\
&= 6 \text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence,

$$
\boxed{\text{Internal diameter of the sphere}=6 \text{ cm}}
$$
