# Exercise 7.4

## Question 5:

Seenu’s house has an overhead tank in the shape of a cylinder. This is filled by pumping water from a sump (underground tank) which is in the shape of a cuboid. The sump has dimensions 2 m × 1.5 m × 1 m. The overhead tank has its radius of 60 cm and height 105 cm. Find the volume of the water left in the sump after the overhead tank has been completely filled with water from the sump which has been full, initially.

---

## Procedure:

1. Find the volume of water in the sump.
2. Find the volume of the overhead cylindrical tank.
3. Subtract the volume of the overhead tank from the volume of the sump.
4. Find the volume of water left in the sump.

---

## Solution:

Given,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Length of sump}
&= 2 \text{ m}
\\
\text{Breadth of sump}
&= 1.5 \text{ m}
\\
\text{Height of sump}
&= 1 \text{ m}
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of the sump,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_1
&= l\times b\times h
\\
&= 2\times1.5\times1
\\
&= 3 \text{ m}^3
\end{aligned}
$$

For the overhead tank,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Radius}
&= 60 \text{ cm}
\\
&= 0.6 \text{ m}
\\
\text{Height}
&= 105 \text{ cm}
\\
&= 1.05 \text{ m}
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of the cylindrical tank,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_2
&= \pi r^2 h
\\
&= \frac{22}{7}\times(0.6)^2\times1.05
\\
&= \frac{22}{7}\times0.36\times1.05
\\
&= 1.188 \text{ m}^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of water left in the sump,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V
&= V_1 - V_2
\\
&= 3 - 1.188
\\
&= 1.812 \text{ m}^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\text{Volume of water left in the sump}=1.812 \text{ m}^3}
$$
