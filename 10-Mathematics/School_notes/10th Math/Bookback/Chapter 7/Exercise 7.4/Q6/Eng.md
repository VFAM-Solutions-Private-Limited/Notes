# Exercise 7.4

## Question 6:

The internal and external diameter of a hollow hemispherical shell are 6 cm and 10 cm respectively. If it is melted and recast into a solid cylinder of diameter 14 cm, then find the height of the cylinder.

---

## Procedure:

1. Find the internal and external radii of the hollow hemispherical shell.
2. Calculate the volume of the material in the hollow hemispherical shell.
3. Find the radius of the solid cylinder.
4. Equate the volume of the shell to the volume of the cylinder.
5. Find the height of the cylinder.

---

## Solution:

Given,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Internal diameter}
&= 6 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{External diameter}
&= 10 \text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Internal radius }(r)
&= \frac{6}{2}
\\
&= 3 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{External radius }(R)
&= \frac{10}{2}
\\
&= 5 \text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Diameter of the cylinder,

$$
14 \text{ cm}
$$

Hence,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Radius of cylinder}
&= 7 \text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of material in the hollow hemispherical shell,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_1
&=
\frac{2}{3}\pi(R^3-r^3)
\\
&=
\frac{2}{3}\pi(5^3-3^3)
\\
&=
\frac{2}{3}\pi(125-27)
\\
&=
\frac{196}{3}\pi
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of the solid cylinder,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_2
&=
\pi r^2 h
\\
&=
\pi(7)^2h
\\
&=
49\pi h
\end{aligned}
$$

Since the shell is melted and recast into a cylinder,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{196}{3}\pi
&=
49\pi h
\\
\frac{196}{3}
&=
49h
\\
h
&=
\frac{196}{147}
\\
&=
\frac{4}{3}
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\text{Height of the cylinder}=\frac{4}{3}\text{ cm}}
$$

or

$$
\boxed{\text{Height of the cylinder}=1.33\text{ cm}}
$$
