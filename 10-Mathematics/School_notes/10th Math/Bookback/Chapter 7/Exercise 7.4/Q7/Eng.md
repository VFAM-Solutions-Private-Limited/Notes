# Exercise 7.4

## Question 7:

A solid sphere of radius 6 cm is melted into a hollow cylinder of uniform thickness. If the external radius of the base of the cylinder is 5 cm and its height is 32 cm, then find the thickness of the cylinder.

---

## Procedure:

1. Find the volume of the solid sphere.
2. Let the internal radius of the hollow cylinder be \(r\) cm.
3. Find the volume of the material in the hollow cylinder.
4. Equate the volume of the sphere and the hollow cylinder.
5. Find the thickness of the cylinder.

---

## Solution:

Given,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Radius of the sphere}
&= 6 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{External radius of the cylinder }(R)
&= 5 \text{ cm}
\\
\text{Height of the cylinder}
&= 32 \text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of the solid sphere,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_1
&=
\frac{4}{3}\pi r^3
\\
&=
\frac{4}{3}\pi(6)^3
\\
&=
288\pi
\end{aligned}
$$

Let \(r\) cm be the internal radius of the hollow cylinder.

Volume of the material in the hollow cylinder,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_2
&=
\pi(R^2-r^2)h
\\
&=
\pi(5^2-r^2)(32)
\\
&=
32\pi(25-r^2)
\end{aligned}
$$

Since the sphere is melted and recast into the hollow cylinder,

$$
\begin{aligned}
288\pi
&=
32\pi(25-r^2)
\\
288
&=
32(25-r^2)
\\
9
&=
25-r^2
\\
r^2
&=
16
\\
r
&=
4 \text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Thickness of the cylinder,

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Thickness}
&=
R-r
\\
&=
5-4
\\
&=
1 \text{ cm}
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\text{Thickness of the cylinder}=1 \text{ cm}}
$$
