# Exercise 7.4

## Question 8:

A hemispherical bowl is filled to the brim with juice. The juice is poured into a cylindrical vessel whose radius is 50% more than its height. If the diameter is same for both the bowl and the cylinder then find the percentage of juice that can be transferred from the bowl into the cylindrical vessel.

---
## Solution:

Given,

The diameter of the hemispherical bowl and the cylindrical vessel are the same.

Let

$$
r=\text{common radius}
$$

The radius of the cylinder is 50% more than its height.

Therefore,

$$
\begin{aligned}
r
&= h+\frac{50}{100}h
\\
&= \frac{3}{2}h
\end{aligned}
$$

Hence,

$$
h=\frac{2r}{3}
$$

Volume of the hemispherical bowl,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_1
&=
\frac{2}{3}\pi r^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Volume of the cylindrical vessel,

$$
\begin{aligned}
V_2
&=
\pi r^2 h
\\
&=
\pi r^2\left(\frac{2r}{3}\right)
\\
&=
\frac{2}{3}\pi r^3
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
V_1=V_2
$$

Hence, the cylindrical vessel can hold all the juice from the hemispherical bowl.

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{Percentage of juice transferred}
&=
\frac{V_2}{V_1}\times100
\\
&=
\frac{\frac{2}{3}\pi r^3}{\frac{2}{3}\pi r^3}\times100
\\
&=100
\end{aligned}
$$

Therefore,

$$
\boxed{\text{Percentage of juice transferred}=100\%}
$$
